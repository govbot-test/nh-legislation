ANACT prohibiting the use of animal testing when other comparable methods are
available.
BeitEnactedbytheSenateandHouseofRepresentativesinGeneralCourtconvened:
1 1 NewChapter;UseofAnimalsinProductTesting. AmendRSAbyinsertingafterchapter359-
2 Uthefollowingnewchapter:
3 CHAPTER359-V
4 USEOFANIMALSINPRODUCTTESTING
5 359-V:1 Definitions. Inthischapter:
6 I. "Alternative test method or strategy” means a test method, including a new or revised
7 method,that:
8 (a) Doesnotuseanimals;
9 (b) Provides information of equivalent or better scientific quality and relevance
10 comparedtotraditionalanimaltestmethods;and
11 (c) Includes, but is not limited to, computational toxicology and bioinformatics, high-
12 throughput screening methods, testing of categories of chemical substances, tiered testing methods,
13 invitrostudies,andmicrophysiologicalsystems.
14 II. "Animal"meansanylivingvertebrateotherthanhumans.
15 III. “Biomedical research” means the investigation of the biological processes and causes of
16 disease or research conducted to increase fundamental scientific knowledge or to expand the
17 understandingabout how processes inlivingorganisms develop and function. “Biomedical research”
18 shall not include testing done to assess the safety or efficacy of chemicals, ingredients, drugs,
19 vaccines,productformulations,orproducts.
20 IV. "Testing facility" means any institution, business, partnership, corporation, association,
21 or other entity that conducts research, studies, tests, or experiments to assess chemicals,
22 ingredients, drugs, vaccines, product formulations, or products in the state. Testing facility does not
23 includeaninstitutionofhighereducationasdefinedinRSA188-H:1.
24 V. "Traditional animal test method" means a process, procedure, or experiment using
25 animals toobtain informationon thecharacteristics of a chemical, ingredient, drug, vaccine, product
26 formulation, or product and that generates information regarding the ability of the chemical,
27 ingredient, drug, vaccine, product formulation or product to produce a specific biological effect under
28 specifiedconditions.
29 359-V:2 AnimalTestingProhibitedUnderCertainCircumstances.
SB444-FN-ASAMENDEDBYTHESENATE
-Page2-
1 I.(a) No testingfacilityinthestateshall useatraditional animal testmethod inthis stateif
2 an agency responsible for regulating the specific product or activity for which the test method is
3 beingusedhaseither:
4 (1) Approvedanalternativetestmethodorstrategy;or
5 (2) Madeavailableorgrantedawaiverfromusingatraditionalanimaltestmethod.
6 (b) Where there is no alternative test method or strategy and no waiver available, a
7 testing facility that employs a traditional animal test method shall employ a method involving the
8 fewest possible number of animals and that reduces the level of pain, suffering, and stress of each
9 animalsubjectedtotesting.
10 (c) No provision of this section shall be construed to apply to any animal research
11 conductedforthepurposesofbiomedicalresearch.
12 (d) Thischaptershallnotprohibittheuseofanynonanimaltestmethodorstrategythat
13 isnotdefinedinRSA359-V:1.
14 (e) This chapter shall not prohibit the use of traditional animal test methods to comply
15 withrequirementsofstateorfederalagencies.
16 (f) Thischaptershallnotprohibittheuseoftraditionalanimaltestmethods,ifneededto
17 comply with a written request from a state or federal agency in circumstances where the agency has
18 approved an alternative test method or strategy but has also expressly concluded that a traditional
19 animaltestmethodisneededtofullyassesstheimpactsonthehealthorsafetyofconsumers.
20 II. Each testing facility in the state shall report annually to the governor's commission on
21 the humane treatment of animals regarding any traditional animal test method or alternative test
22 method or strategy conducted in the prior calendar year. Such report shall include the number and
23 species of animal used, the type and number of alternative test methods or strategies used, the
24 number of waivers used, and the reason for using any traditional animal test methods, alternative
25 test methods or strategies, and waivers, including an explanation for why a traditional animal test
26 method was required. The governor's commission on the humane treatment of animals shall make
27 the results of this data collection publicly available no later than 90 days after receiving the report.
28 Testing facilities shall submit the report no later than January 20, or the next business day
29 following,beginningonJanuary20,2028.
30 359-V:3 Severability. If any provision of this chapter is held invalid, or if its application to any
31 personorcircumstanceisheldinvalid,suchinvaliditydoesnotaffectotherprovisionsorapplications
32 ofthischapterwhichcanbegiveneffectwithouttheinvalidprovisionorapplication.
33 2 EffectiveDate. ThisactshalltakeeffectJanuary1,2027.
