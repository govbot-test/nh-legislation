ANACT relativetoshort-term,limiteddurationhealthinsurancepolicies.
SPONSORS: Sen. Innis, Dist 7; Sen. McGough, Dist 11; Rep. Labrie, Hills. 2; Rep. Kuttab, Rock.
17;Rep.Miles,Hills.12
COMMITTEE: HealthandHumanServices
─────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
AMENDEDANALYSIS
This bill allows insurers to offer short-term, limited duration health insurance policies of less than 12
months and not more than 36 months. This bill also requires insurers to provide written notice to
consumersthatsuchplansarenotrequiredtocomplywiththeACA,mayexcludepre-existingconditions,
andmayincludetermsthatdifferfromcomprehensivehealthplans.

13 saleofplansundersubparagraph(a),aninsurershallprovideaclearandconspicuousnoticetothe
14 consumer,duringtheapplicationprocessandinthecontract,thatcontainsthefollowinglanguage:
15 “Short-termlimiteddurationhealthplansarenotrequiredtocomplywithregulationsunderthefederal
16 PatientProtectionandAffordableCareAct,arenotconsideredMinimumEssentialCoverage,andmay
17 containlimitationsonorexcludecoverageforpre-existingconditions. Theplanalsomaycontainbenefit
18 maximums,out-of-pocketcosts,andlimitationsonrenewabilitythatdifferfromcomprehensivehealth
19 plans.Consumersshouldreviewthepolicycarefullytounderstandthetermsandcomparepotentialcosts
20 andcoverageswithplansavailableforpurchaseonHealthcare.govordirectlyfromahealthcareinsurer.”
199:2 EffectiveDate. ThisactshalltakeeffectJanuary1,2027.
