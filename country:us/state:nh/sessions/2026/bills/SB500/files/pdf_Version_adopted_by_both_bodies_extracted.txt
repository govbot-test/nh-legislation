ANACT relativetorestroomaccessforcertaincommercialmotorvehicleoperators.
BeitEnactedbytheSenateandHouseofRepresentativesinGeneralCourtconvened:
1 1 New Subdivision; Restroom Access for Drivers. Amend RSA 155 by inserting after section 82
2 thefollowingnewsubdivision:
3 RestroomAccessforDrivers
4 155:82-a RestroomAccessforCoveredDrivers.
5 I. Asusedinthissection:
6 (a) "Covereddriver"meansanycommercialdriverasdefinedinRSA259:12.
7 (b) "Covered establishment" means a place of business open to the general public for the
8 saleof goods or services; or ashipper, receiver, manufacturer, warehouse, distributioncenter, or any
9 other business entity that is receiving or sending goods by commercial motor vehicle. "Covered
10 establishment" does not include any structure such as a filling station, service station, or restaurant
11 of 800 square feet or less that has a restroom located within such structure that is only intended for
12 usebyemployees.
13 (c) "Covered restroom facility" means a restroom located on the premises of a covered
14 establishment that is intended for use by customers or employees of the establishment and that is
15 located in an area where providing access would not create an obvious health or safety risk to a
16 covereddriverandwouldnotposeanobvioussecurityrisktothecoveredestablishment.
17 II. A covered driver shall be granted access to any covered restroom facility at any covered
18 establishment to which such employee delivers any goods or cargo or is waiting to be loaded to
19 transportgoodsorcargo.
20 III. Nothing in this section shall be construed to require a covered establishment to make
21 anyphysicalchangestoarestroomtobeincompliancewiththissection.
22 155:82-b RestroomAccessforCoveredDrayageTruckDrivers.
23 I. Inthissection:
24 (a) “Covereddrayagetruckoperator”meansthedriverofanyin-useon-roadvehiclewith
25 a gross vehicle weight rating of greater than 33,000 pounds operating on or transgressing through
26 port or intermodal rail yard property for the purpose of loading, unloading, or transporting cargo,
27 includingcontainerized,bulk,orbreak-bulkgoods.
28 (b) “Coveredrestroom”meansarestroomorportablechemicaltoiletthatislocatedinan
29 areathatdoesnotposeanobvioushealthorsafetyrisktoacovereddrayagetruckoperator.
SB500-VERSIONADOPTEDBYBOTHBODIES
-Page2-
1 (c) “Terminal operator" means the business entity operating a marine terminal for
2 loading and unloading cargo to and from marine vessels, and includes the port authority if the port
3 isdirectlyoperatingthemarineterminalinloadingandunloadingcargotoandfrommarinevessels.
4 II. A terminal operator shall provide a sufficient number of covered restrooms for use by
5 covereddrayagetruckoperatorsinareasoftheterminalthatsuchoperatorstypicallyhaveaccessto,
6 byproviding:
7 (a) Access to existing restrooms while covered drayage truck operators are on port
8 property and when such access does not pose an obvious safety risk to such truck operators and
9 otheremployeesoftheterminaloperatorinthearea;
10 (b) Additionalrestrooms,ifnecessary,atlocationswherethereisthemostneed;and
11 (c) A place for covered drayage truck operators to park vehicles while accessing such
12 restrooms.
13 2 EffectiveDate. Thisactshalltakeeffect60daysafteritspassage.
