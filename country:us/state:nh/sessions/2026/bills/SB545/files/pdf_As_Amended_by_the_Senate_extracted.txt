ANALYSIS
This bill removes the resource limit for the Medicare savings program and, subject to CMS
approval, makes the low income subsidy for Medicare Part D available to residents to assist with
prescriptiondrugcosts.

STATEOFNEWHAMPSHIRE
IntheYearofOurLordTwoThousandTwenty-Six
ANACT relativetofinancialeligibilityfortheMedicaresavingsprogram.
BeitEnactedbytheSenateandHouseofRepresentativesinGeneralCourtconvened:
1 1 ShortTitle. Thisactshallbeknownas"TheHelpforLowIncomeSeniorsActof2026".
2 2 New Section; Financial Eligibility for Medicare Savings Program. Amend RSA 167 by
3 insertingaftersection4-fthefollowingnewsection:
4 167:4-g MedicareSavingsProgram.
5 I. The department shall administer the Medicare savings program as described in 42 U.S.C.
6 section1396a(a)(10)(E)inaccordancewithfederallawandthissection.
7 II. Financial eligibility for the Medicare savings program shall, subject to CMS approval,
8 make the low income subsidy for Medicare Part D available to residents to assist with prescription
9 drugcosts.
10 3 EffectiveDate. Thisactshalltakeeffect60daysafteritspassage.

SB545-FN-FISCALNOTE
ASAMENDEDBYTHESENATE(AMENDMENT#2026-1210s)
ANACT relativetofinancialeligibilityfortheMedicaresavingsprogram.
FISCALIMPACT:
This bill requires the Department of Health and Human Services to make the low-income
subsidy for Medicare Part D available to residents who meet the financial eligibility criteria for
participation in the Medicare savings program. The Depatment notes that the subsidy is
already available to such residents, although there is currently no requirement in either state
orfederallawthatthisbethecase. Thesubsidyisadministeredbythefederalgovernmentand
is fully federally funded, provided that expenditures do not exceed the state's allocation of
federal funds available for this purpose. The Department notes that from calendar years 2021
through 2025, subsidy expenditures have ranged from 55 percent to 67 percent of the state's
allotment. Forthisreason,theDepartmentassumesthatthebillwillhavenofiscalimpact.
