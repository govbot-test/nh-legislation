AMENDEDANALYSIS
This bill requires all school employees to respond honestly and completely to written requests by
parents and legal guardians regarding information relating to their children, except where such
responsesareprohibitedbystateorfederallaw.

STATEOFNEWHAMPSHIRE
IntheYearofOurLordTwoThousandTwenty-Six
ANACT relative to mandatory disclosure by school district employees to parents and legal
guardians.
BeitEnactedbytheSenateandHouseofRepresentativesinGeneralCourtconvened:
1 1 ShortTitle. ThisactshallbeknownastheHonestyandTransparencyinEducationAct.
2 2 New Section; Communications Between Teachers, Parents, and Legal Guardians. Amend
3 RSA189byinsertingaftersection13-dthefollowingnewsection:
4 189:13-e CommunicationsBetweenTeachers,Parents,andLegalGuardians.
5 I. Educators credentialed by the New Hampshire department of education shall have a duty
6 to respond to written inquiries by parents and legal guardians regarding information relating to
7 theirchildenrolledinthateducator’sschool. Suchresponsetoawrittenrequestshallbesentwithin
8 10 business days of its receipt and be answered completely and honestly, except where prohibited by
9 stateorfederallaw.
10 II. If, in the good faith determination of the credential holder, such complete and honest
11 response to a request would put the child at imminent risk of abuse or neglect, as defined in RSA
12 169-C:3, such credential holder shall file a report with the superintendent, and the request for
13 informationshallbedeniedinwriting.
14 III. On or before June 30, 2027, the state board of education shall adopt rules, pursuant to
15 RSA 541-A, amending the code of ethics and the code of conduct for New Hampshire educators
16 relativetothissection.
17 IV. Violation of this section shall be considered a violation of the code of conduct for New
18 Hampshire educators and subject to investigation, in accordance with administrative rules, against
19 thatcredentialholder.
20 3 EffectiveDate. ThisactshalltakeeffectJanuary1,2027.
