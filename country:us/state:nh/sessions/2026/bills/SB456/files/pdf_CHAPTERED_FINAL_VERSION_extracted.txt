ANACT establishingthecommissionforchildren'sfutures.
BeitEnactedbytheSenateandHouseofRepresentativesinGeneralCourtconvened:
1 101:1 NewSubdivision;StudyCommissionforChildren'sFutures. AmendRSA200byinsertingafter
2 section63thefollowingnewsubdivision:
3 StudyCommissionforChildren'sFutures
4 200:64 Commission Established. There is a commission established to study the health and well-
5 being of children in the state and to identify the gaps in mental and physical health services provided to
6 children. Thecommissionshallbeadministrativelyattachedtothedepartmentofeducation.
7 I. NotwithstandingRSA14:49,themembersofthecommissionshallbeasfollows:
8 (a) Two members of the house of representatives, appointed by the speaker of the house of
9 representatives;
10 (b) Onememberofthesenatefromthedemocraticpartyandonememberofthesenatefrom
11 therepublicanparty,appointedbythepresidentofthesenate;
12 (c) Twohealthcareprofessionalslicensedinthestate,appointedbythegovernor;
13 (d) One medical practitioner licensed in the state specializing in either traditional or holistic
14 medicine,appointedbythespeakerofthehouse;
15 (e) Onechildpsychologistlicensedinthestate,appointedbythesenatepresident;
16 (f) Onementalhealthcareprovider,appointedbythespeakerofthehouse;
17 (g) Twoteacherscertifiedbythedepartmentofeducation,oneofwhomiscertifiedinspecial
18 education,appointedbythegovernor;
19 (h) Onedietitian,asdefinedinRSA326-H:3,appointedbythesenatepresident;
20 (i) One member of state or local law enforcement, appointed by the speaker of the house;
21 and
22 (j) Threeparentsof childrenresidinginthestate, oneof whom isappointedbythe governor,
23 one of whom is appointed by the speaker of the house, and one of whom is appointed by the senate
24 president.
25 II. Legislative members of the commission shall receive mileage at the legislative rate when
26 attendingtothedutiesofthecommission.
27 III. Thecommissionshallstudy:
28 (a) Theusageof individualizededucationprograms(IEPs) in schoolsandtrendswithin IEPs
29 overtime;
30 (b) Thechildusagerateofpharmaceuticalswithpsychoactiveeffects;
31 (c) Reportsofdiagnosedmentalhealthissuesinyouth;

1 (d) The proportion of students that know of someone who has experienced a mental health
2 crisis;
3 (e) Changesintheprevalenceofchildhoodcancerandlong-termdiseases;
4 (f) Changesinautismspectrumdisorderandattentiondeficitdisorderdiagnoses;
5 (g) Theamountofmentalhealthservicesprovidedtominorsandassociatedtrendswithsuch
6 services;
7 (h) The prevalence of criminal street gangs, as defined in RSA 651:6, I-a(c) in New
8 Hampshire;
9 (i) The number of incidents of violence in public schools and the impact such incidents have
10 onstudents;
11 (j) Truancyandattendanceratesinpublicschools;
12 (k) The prevalence of adverse childhood experiences and the effect such experiences have
13 onchildren;
14 (l) Childhoodexposuretopornographiccontent;and
15 (m) Youthhomelessnessrates.
16 IV. The members of the study commission shall elect a chairperson from among the members.
17 The first meeting of the commission shall be called by the first-named house member. The first meeting
18 ofthecommissionshall beheldwithin45daysof theeffectivedateof thissection. Eight membersof the
19 commissionshallconstituteaquorum.
20 V. Thecommissionshall report its findingsandanyrecommendationsfor proposedlegislation to
21 thespeakerofthehouseofrepresentatives,thepresidentofthesenate,thehouseclerk,thesenateclerk,
