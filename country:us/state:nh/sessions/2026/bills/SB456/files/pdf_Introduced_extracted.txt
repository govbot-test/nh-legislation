─────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
ANALYSIS
This bill establishes a commission to study issues related to the public health and safety of
childreninthestate.

SB456-ASINTRODUCED
26-2227
12/09
STATEOFNEWHAMPSHIRE
IntheYearofOurLordTwoThousandTwenty-Six
ANACT establishingthecommissionforchildren'sfutures.
BeitEnactedbytheSenateandHouseofRepresentativesinGeneralCourtconvened:
1 1 New Subdivision; Study Commission for Children's Futures. Amend RSA 200 by inserting
2 aftersection63thefollowingnewsubdivision:
3 StudyCommissionforChildren'sFutures
4 200:64 Commission Established. There is a commission established to study the health and
5 well-being of children in the state and to identify the gaps in mental and physical health services
6 providedtochildren.
7 I. NotwithstandingRSA14:49,themembersofthecommissionshallbeasfollows:
8 (a) Two members of the house of representatives, appointed by the speaker of the house
9 ofrepresentatives.
10 (b) One member of the senate from the democratic party and one member of the senate
11 fromtherepublicanparty,appointedbythepresidentofthesenate.
12 (c) Seventeen members appointed by the governor, including but not limited to the
13 followinggroups:
14 (1) Healthcareprofessionalslicensedinthestate;
15 (2) Medical practitioners licensed in the state specializing in either traditional or
16 holisticmedicine;
17 (3) Childpsychologistslicensedinthestate;
18 (4) Mentalhealthcareproviders;
19 (5) Teacherscertifiedbythedepartmentofeducation;
20 (6) Dietitians,asdefinedinRSA326-H:3;
21 (7) Membersofstateorlocallawenforcement;
22 (8) Statelegislators;and
23 (9) Parentsofchildrenresidinginthestate.
24 II. Legislative members of the commission shall receive mileage at the legislative rate when
25 attendingtothedutiesofthecommission.
26 III. Thecommissionshallstudy:
27 (a) The usage of individualized education programs (IEPs) in schools and trends within
28 IEPsovertime;
29 (b) Thechildusagerateofpharmaceuticalswithpsychoactiveeffects;
30 (c) Reportsofdiagnosedmentalhealthissuesinyouth;
SB456 -ASINTRODUCED
-Page2-
1 (d) The proportion of students that know of someone who has experienced a mental
2 healthcrisis;
3 (e) Changesintheprevalenceofchildhoodcancerandlong-termdiseases;
4 (f) Changesinautismspectrumdisorderandattentiondeficitdisorderdiagnoses;
5 (g) The amount of mental health services provided to minors and associated trends with
6 suchservices;
7 (h) The prevalence of criminal street gangs, as defined in RSA 651:6, I-a(c) in New
8 Hampshire;
9 (i) The number of incidents of violence in public schools and the impact such incidents
10 haveonstudents;
11 (j) Truancyandattendanceratesinpublicschools;
12 (k) Theprevalenceofadversechildhoodexperiencesandtheeffectsuchexperienceshave
13 onchildren;
14 (l) Childhoodexposuretopornographiccontent;and
15 (m) Youthhomelessnessrates.
16 IV. The members of the study commission shall elect a chairperson from among the
17 members. The first meeting of the commission shall be called by the first-named house member.
18 The first meeting of the commission shall be held within 45 days of the effective date of this section.
19 Elevenmembersofthecommissionshallconstituteaquorum.
20 V. The commission shall report its findings and any recommendations for proposed
21 legislation to the speaker of the house of representatives, the president of the senate, the house
22 clerk,thesenateclerk,thegovernor,andthestatelibraryannuallyonNovember1ofeachyear.
23 2 EffectiveDate. Thisactshalltakeeffectuponitspassage.
