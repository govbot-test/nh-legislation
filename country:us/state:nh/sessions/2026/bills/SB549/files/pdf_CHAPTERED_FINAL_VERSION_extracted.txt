STATEOFNEWHAMPSHIRE
IntheYearofOurLordTwoThousandTwenty-Six
ANACT requiring certain syringe service program entities to provide options for disposal of
usedsyringesandneedlesandcreatingreportingrequirementsforsuchentities.
BeitEnactedbytheSenateandHouseofRepresentativesinGeneralCourtconvened:
1 106:1 New Paragraph; Pharmacists and Pharmacies; Regulation of Pharmacies; Syringe Service
2 Programs;AuthorizedActivitiesandFundingSources. AmendRSA318:43-abyinsertingafterparagraph
3 IIthefollowingnewparagraph:
4 II-a.(a) Any entity operating a syringe service program in New Hampshire that involves the
5 provision of sterile needles and syringes shall provide options for the disposal of used syringes, needles,
6 andotherrelatedparaphernalia. Suchoptionsshallbefreetothepublic.
7 (b) Any organization required to provide options for the disposal of used syringes, needles,
8 and other related paraphernalia shall undertake an annual marketing campaign to inform participants of
9 freedisposaloptions.
10 (c) At the time of a quarterly report, each entity operating a syringe service program in New
11 Hampshire shall report to the department whether they are required to provide options for disposal as
12 outlinedinthisparagraphand,ifso,shalldemonstratetheircompliance.
13 (d) At the time of a quarterly report, each entity operating a syringe service program in New
14 Hampshire shall report to the department calculated return rates. In addition to calculated return rates,
15 eachprogramshallprovideadescriptionofmethodsusedinsyringecountingorestimation;separationof
16 returns from direct participant exchange versus secondary/community sources; and a narrative of factors
17 affecting performance and improvement efforts. Strengthening syringe services programs (SSPs) shall
18 seek to achieve and maintain a return rate of 95 percent or higher in each calendar quarter. The
19 department shall assess performance over rolling 4-quarter periods. A return rate may exceed 100
20 percent. If an SSP reports a return rate below 95 percent for 2 consecutive quarters, it must submit a
21 corrective action plan within 30 days. The plan shall include strategies to increase return rate, such as
22 enhanced drop box access, peer outreach, incentives, or community partnerships; a timeline with
23 measurabletargets; anda statement of resourceor technical needs. Upon request, thedepartment shall
24 provide support, including guidance on data gathering and best practices. The department shall adopt
25 rules pursuant to RSA 541-A establishing a schedule of fines for failure to file required reports or
26 implementcorrectiveactionplans.
27 (e) No municipality shall adopt any local ordinance or other rule to prohibit any activity
28 authorizedbythisparagraph.
29 106:2 EffectiveDate. Thisactshalltakeeffect90daysafteritspassage.
Approved:May28,2026
EffectiveDate:August26,2026
