ANACT requiring certain syringe service program entities to provide options for disposal
of used syringes and needles and creating reporting requirements for such
entities.
BeitEnactedbytheSenateandHouseofRepresentativesinGeneralCourtconvened:
1 1 New Paragraph; Pharmacists and Pharmacies; Regulation of Pharmacies; Syringe Service
2 Programs; Authorized Activities and Funding Sources. Amend RSA 318:43-a by inserting after
3 paragraphIIthefollowingnewparagraph:
4 II-a.(a) Any entity operating a syringe service program in New Hampshire that involves the
5 provision of sterile needles and syringes shall provide options for the disposal of used syringes,
6 needles,andotherrelatedparaphernalia. Suchoptionsshallbefreetothepublic.
7 (b) Any organization required to provide options for the disposal of used syringes,
8 needles, and other related paraphernalia shall undertake an annual marketing campaign to inform
9 participantsoffreedisposaloptions.
10 (c) At the time of a quarterly report, each entity operating a syringe service program in
11 New Hampshire shall report to the department whether they are required to provide options for
12 disposalasoutlinedinthisparagraphand,ifso,shalldemonstratetheircompliance.
13 (d) At the time of a quarterly report, each entity operating a syringe service program in
14 New Hampshire shall report to the department calculated return rates. In addition to calculated
15 return rates, each program shall provide a description of methods used in syringe counting or
16 estimation; separation of returns from direct participant exchange versus secondary/community
17 sources; and a narrative of factors affecting performance and improvement efforts. Strengthening
18 syringe services programs (SSPs) shall seek to achieve and maintain a return rate of 95 percent or
19 higher in each calendar quarter. The department shall assess performance over rolling 4-quarter
20 periods. Areturnratemayexceed 100percent. IfanSSPreports areturnratebelow 95percent for
21 2 consecutive quarters, it must submit a corrective action plan within 30 days. The plan shall
22 include strategies to increase return rate, such as enhanced drop box access, peer outreach,
23 incentives, or community partnerships; a timeline with measurable targets; and a statement of
24 resource or technical needs. Upon request, the department shall provide support, including
25 guidance on data gathering and best practices. The department shall adopt rules pursuant to RSA
26 541-A establishing a schedule of fines for failure to file required reports or implement corrective
27 actionplans.
28 (e) No municipality shall adopt any local ordinance or other rule to prohibit any activity
29 authorizedbythisparagraph.

METHODOLOGY:
Thisbillrequiresallsyringeserviceprograms(SSP)thatdistributesterilesyringesto:
(1)providefreedisposaloptionsforusedsyringesandrelatedparaphernalia;
(2)conductanannualmarketingcampaignregardingdisposaloptions;
(3)submitquarterlyreportsdocumentingcompliancewithdisposalrequirements;and
(4) report syringe return rates and associated methodology, narrative context, and performance
factors.
Inaddition,thebill establishesa95percentreturnrategoal,requirescorrectiveactionplansfor
programs falling below this threshold for two consecutive quarters, and directs the Department
of Health and Human Services to provide technical assistance upon request. The Department
must alsoassess performanceover rollingfour quarter periods and adopt administrativerules to
establish a schedule of fines for non-compliance. The Department states these new, ongoing
oversight responsibilities will result in the need for a new, full-time program specialist position,
atanestimatedcostof$115,000inFY27,$120,000inFY28,and$126,000inFY29.
To the extent that any local funds are budgeted for SSPs, the fiscal impact on municipalities is
indeterminable.
