ANACT relative to port electrification, microgrid development, and cybersecurity standards for
energyandwatersystems.
BeitEnactedbytheSenateandHouseofRepresentativesinGeneralCourtconvened:
1 197:1 Statement of Purpose. The purpose of this act is to promote New Hampshire’s energy
2 resilience and deployment of technologies supporting marine shipping, microgrid development, and
3 cybersecurityfordistributedenergyresources.
4 197:2 New Paragraph; Energy Infrastructure Development and Corridors; Definition of Microgrid
5 Added. AmendRSA162-R:1byinsertingafterparagraphIIIthefollowingnewparagraph:
6 III-a. "Microgrid" means a group of interconnected loads and distributed energy resources acting
7 as a single controllable entity with respect to the grid, with the ability to connect/disconnect to operate in
8 grid-connected or island mode, improve reliability and resilience to grid disturbances, and enable local
9 assets to collaborate to save costs, run during interruptions, or support remote operations. A microgrid
10 may also feature system modeling/simulation, power electronic converters and control algorithms,
11 controller hardware-in-the-loop testing, power hardware-in-the-loop testing, programmable AC/DC power
12 supplies for grid/PV/battery emulation, and hybrid microgrid testing including distribution integration of
13 windturbines,PV,dynamometers,loads,andenergystorage.
14 197:3 New Section; Port Electrification Task Force. Amend RSA 162-R by inserting after section 6
15 thefollowingnewsection:
16 162-R:7 PortElectrificationReport.
17 I. Thereisherebyestablishedataskforcetoreportonportelectrification.
18 II. Thetaskforceshallconsistof:
19 (a) The director of the division of ports and harbors, or designee, who shall serve as chair
20 andconveningauthorityandshalladministerthetaskforce.
21 (b) Thecommissionerofthedepartmentoftransportation,ordesignee.
22 (c) Thecommissionerofthedepartmentofenergy,ordesignee.
23 (d) Thecommissionerofthedepartmentofbusinessandeconomicaffairs,ordesignee.
24 III. Thereportshall:
25 (a) Identify feasible technologies and locations for charging infrastructure, energy supply,
26 includingon-sitegeneration,andenergystorageunits,toservehybridelectric,andfuelcellelectricships,
27 forshipping,fisheries,andothervessels,andportsidepowersupplyforconventional-fueledvessels.
28 (b) Assessapplicablestate,federal,andinternationalregulationsandregulatorybarriers.
29 (c) Identifyassociatedcosts,fundingsources,andpotentialmodelsforprivatedevelopment.
30 (d) Identifytransmissionandinterconnectionsiting.

1 IV. The report required by this section shall be completed on or before June 30, 2027. Copies
2 shall be submitted to the governor, speaker of the house, president of the senate, and the chairs of the
3 houseandsenatestandingcommitteesfortransportation,energy,andcommerce.
4 197:4 MicrogridInvestigationPhaseII.
5 I. Within 120 days of the effective date of this section, the department of energy shall initiate a
6 proceeding to continue to investigate the potential benefits, risks, and key considerations around
7 developing a framework for electricity microgrids in the state, continuing the work begun in Inv 2024-001.
8 In so doing, the department shall consult with electric distribution utilities, microgrid technology
9 companies, distributed generation project developers, high density load businesses, and any other
10 relevantentities.
11 II. The department’s investigative proceeding shall examine and make recommendations
12 concerning:
13 (a) Thefeasibilityoffinancingoptionsformicrogrids,includingC-PACER.
14 (b) Identifyingpoliticalsubdivisionsandindustrialusersopentomicrogriddevelopment.
15 (c) Development of an approach to use the “sandbox” methodology to enhance the
16 regulatoryprocess.
17 (d) Cybersecuritystandardsformicrogridsandotherdistributedenergyresources.
18 (e) Theneedforlegislationorregulatoryrelieftoallowforpilotprojects.
19 (f) Evaluationcriteriaforapilotproject.
20 (g) Anyotherrelevantissueraisedinthedepartment’sinvestigativeproceedings.
21 III. Thedepartment shall report itsfindings andrecommendationstothelegislaturenolater than
22 oneyearafterinitiatingthestudy.
23 IV. Forthepurposesofthissection,"microgrid"shallhavethesamemeaningasinRSA162-R:1,
24 III-a.
25 197:5 CybersecurityandElectricGridInterconnectionGuidelines.
26 I. Within 120 days of the effective date of this section, the department of energy shall initiate a
27 proceeding, in consultation as deemed necessary with the governor’s advisor for utility critical
28 infrastructure cybersecurity, the managing director of the New England Utility Cybersecurity Integration
29 Collaborative, distributed energy resources businesses, electric distribution utilities, and regional/federal
30 transmissionauthorities,todetermine:
31 (a) The need for guidelines or standards for cybersecurity standards for distributed energy
32 generationandrelateddevices.
33 (b) The proper venue for promulgation of those guidelines or standards, including, but not
34 limitedto,rulemaking,statutorychanges,andincludingguidelinesorstandardswithinexistingprocedures
35 andfilings.
36 II. Suchnewguidelinesorstandardsshallnotapplytogenerationandrelateddeviceslicensedor
37 regulatedbytheFederalEnergyRegulatoryCommission.
38 III. The department shall report its findings and recommendations to the legislature no later than
39 one year after initiating the study. The report shall include recommendations for statutory changes,

1 rulemaking,orotheractionsasneeded. Thedepartmentshallsubmitcopiesofthereporttothegovernor,
2 senatepresident,speakerofthehouse,andchairsofsenateandhouseenergypolicycommittees.
3 197:6 New Section; Cybersecurity Protection Program; Public Water Systems. Amend RSA 485 by
4 insertingaftersection3-dthefollowingnewsection:
5 485:3-e CybersecurityProtectionProgram.
6 I. Publicwatersystemsshallimplementacybersecurityprotectionprogramthatiscommensurate
7 with the size and complexity of the public water system in accordance with rules adopted by the
8 department.
9 II. Public water systems that do not use an Internet-connected control system are exempt from
10 theprovisionsofthissection.
11 III. In this section, “cybersecurity protection program” means steps to prevent an event from
12 occurringonorbeingconductedthroughacomputernetworkthatjeopardizestheconfidentiality,integrity,
13 or availability of computers, information systems, communications systems, networks, physical or virtual
14 infrastructure controlled by computers or information systems, or the information or processes residing
15 thereon.
16 IV. The commissioner may adopt rules to implement a cybersecurity protection program for
17 publicwatersystems.
18 197:7 New Section; Cybersecurity Protection Program; Wastewater Treatment Facilities. Amend
19 RSA485-Abyinsertingaftersection485-A:5-ethefollowingnewsection:
20 485-A:5-f CybersecurityProtectionProgram.
21 I. Wastewatertreatmentplantsorwastewaterfacilitiesshallimplementacybersecurityprotection
22 program that is commensurate with the size and complexity of the wastewater treatment plant or
23 wastewaterfacilityinaccordancewithrulesadoptedbythedepartment.
24 II. Wastewater treatment plants or wastewater facilities that do not use an Internet- connected
25 controlsystemareexemptfromtheprovisionsofthissection.
26 III. In this section, “cybersecurity protection program” means steps to prevent an event from
27 occurringonorbeingconductedthroughacomputernetworkthatjeopardizestheconfidentiality,integrity,
28 or availability of computers, information systems, communications systems, networks, physical or virtual
29 infrastructure controlled by computers or information systems, or the information or processes residing
30 thereon.
31 IV. The commissioner shall adopt rules to implement a cybersecurity protection program for
32 wastewatertreatmentplantsorwastewaterfacilities.
33 197:8 Repeal. RSA162-R:7,relativetotheportelectrificationtaskforce,isrepealed.

35 I. Section8ofthisactshalltakeeffectJuly1,2027.
36 II. Theremainderofthisactshalltakeeffectuponitspassage.
