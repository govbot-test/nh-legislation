657-FN
ANACT relative to the use of information technology and artificial intelligence systems by
stateagencies.
SPONSORS: Sen. Perkins Kwoka, Dist 21; Sen. Rosenwald, Dist 13; Sen. Prentiss, Dist 5; Sen.
Long, Dist 20; Sen. Watters, Dist 4; Rep. Simpson, Rock. 33; Rep. Telerski, Hills.
11
COMMITTEE: Judiciary
─────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
AMENDEDANALYSIS
Thisbill:
I. Changes the advice the commissioner of the department of information technology receives
fromtheinformationtechnologycouncil.
II. Allowsthecommissionertoestablishanartificialintelligence-relatedadvisementcommittee.
III. Requires the commissioner to submit an annual report detailing state agency use of
artificialintelligencesystems.

ANACT relative to the use of information technology and artificial intelligence systems by
stateagencies.
BeitEnactedbytheSenateandHouseofRepresentativesinGeneralCourtconvened:
1 1 Department of Information Technology; Information Technology Council. RSA 21-R:6, I is
2 repealedandreenactedtoreadasfollows:
3 I. There is established an information technology council to advise the commissioner on the
4 following:
5 (a) Statewidestrategictechnologyplans.
6 (b) Outsourcing relationships, including those involving the purchase, lease, or rent of
7 technologyequipmentandvendorcontractterms.
8 (c) Computersystemsconsolidation.
9 (d) Implementationofcentralizedservices.
10 (e) Changesininformationtechnologyresources.
11 (f) Statewideinformationtechnologypoliciesandstandards.
12 (g) Budgetingforinformationtechnology.
13 (h) The security of data shared with the federal government, methods to improve state
14 data security practices, and suggested legislation, policies, and procedures to limit data sharing
15 whenitsconfidentialitycannotbeassured.
16 (i) The development, procurement, deployment, and use of artificial intelligence systems
17 bystateagencies,including:
18 (1) Compliance with RSA 5-D, the department's code of ethics for artificial
19 intelligence systems, and safeguards for the right of privacy guaranteed by part I, article 2-b of the
20 NewHampshireconstitution;
21 (2) Opportunities to improve government efficiency, transparency, and
22 accountabilitythroughartificialintelligence;and
23 (3) The effects of artificial intelligence on the state workforce, including training
24 needsandchangestoemploymentpatterns.
25 2 Department of Information Technology; Technical Committees. RSA 21-R:7 is repealed and
26 reenactedtoreadasfollows:
27 21-R:7 TechnicalCommittees.
28 I. The commissioner may establish technical committees to advise him or her on technical
29 issuesrelatingto,butnotlimitedto:
SB657-FN-ASAMENDEDBYTHEHOUSE
-Page2-
1 (a) Hardware,software,andtelecommunicationsstandards.
2 (b) Informationtechnologyplanningprocesses.
3 (c) Developmentofstatewidepoliciesandprocedures.
4 (d) EmergingInternetandintranettechnologies.
5 (e) E-governmentstrategyanddeployment.
6 (f) Wideareanetworkefficiencies.
7 (g) Cybersecurity.
8 (h) Cloudtechnologiesorstrategies.
9 (i) Artificial intelligence technologies, including generative artificial intelligence,
10 automateddecisionsystems,andmachinelearning.
11 II. Personnel from all 3 branches of state government with expertise and knowledge in the
12 applicablesubjectareasshallberepresentedoneachcommittee.
13 3 New Section; Department of Information Technology; Artificial Intelligence Inventory and
14 AnnualReport. AmendRSA21-Rbyinsertingaftersection9-athefollowingnewsection:
15 21-R:9-b ArtificialIntelligenceInventoryandAnnualReport.
16 I. The commissioner shall maintain a current inventory of all artificial intelligence systems,
17 as defined in RSA 5-D:1, deployed by executive branch agencies. The inventory shall include, for
18 each system, the name and vendor, the agencies using the system, a brief description of its purpose
19 and function, whether the system is used in making decisions affecting the rights or benefits of
20 individuals, and the date of deployment. The inventory shall be posted on the department's website
21 andupdatednotlessthanannually.
22 II. On or before October 1 of each year, the commissioner, in consultation with the
23 information technology council, shall submit a report to the president of the senate, the speaker of
24 the house of representatives, the senate clerk, the house clerk, the governor, and the state library.
25 The report shall satisfy the annual reporting requirement of RSA 5-D:5, III. The report shall
26 include:
27 (a) A summary of the inventory maintained under paragraph I, including any changes
28 fromtheprioryear;
29 (b) AnassessmentofagencycompliancewithRSA5-D;
30 (c) A summary of efficiency gains, cost savings, or service improvements attributable to
31 artificialintelligencedeployment;
32 (d) Any incidents involving the unauthorized use or disclosure of personal information
33 byanartificialintelligencesystem;
34 (e) Theeffectsofartificialintelligenceonworkforcecompositionandtrainingneeds;and
35 (f) Recommendationsforlegislation,policychanges,oradditionalsafeguards.

1 III. For the first report submitted under this section, the commissioner may limit the
2 inventory and efficiency data to information reasonably available and shall establish baseline
3 metricsagainstwhichsubsequentreportsshallmeasureprogress.
4 4 EffectiveDate. Thisactshalltakeeffect60daysafteritspassage.
