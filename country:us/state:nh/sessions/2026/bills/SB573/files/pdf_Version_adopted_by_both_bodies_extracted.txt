5 167-E:1 Definitions. Inthischapter:
6 I. "Certified public safety comfort dog" means a specifically trained dog that provides
7 support, comfort, and crisis response to individuals, groups, and communities in various settings,
8 such as hospitals, nursing homes, schools, disaster relief areas, emergency response/public safety
9 agencies and other places where people may be experiencing stress, trauma, or crisis. Certified
10 public safety comfort dogs are trained to interact gently and empathetically with people, offering
11 unconditional support and non-judgmental companionship to help alleviate anxiety, reduce stress,
12 improve mood, and enhance overall well-being and resilience. Certified public safety comfort dogs
13 arenotconsideredservicedogsoremotionalsupportanimals.
14 II. "Certified public safety comfort dog handler" means an emergency response or public
15 safety worker who is responsible for the care, training, and supervision of a certified public safety
16 comfort dog during its interactions with individuals, groups, and communities in need of support or
17 crisis response. A certified public safety comfort dog handler shall ensure that it behaves
18 appropriately, follows commands, and maintains a calm and comforting presence in various
19 environments. A certified public safety comfort dog handler shall be trained to understand canine
20 behavior, communication, and emotional support techniques, allowing them to effectively guide the
21 certified public safety comfort dog's interactions with individuals, groups, or communities in distress
22 orcrisissituations.
23 III. "Emergency response or public safety worker" means any law enforcement officer
24 certified under RSA 106-L, certified county corrections officer, sheriff or deputy sheriff, state police
25 officer, civilian law enforcement employee, civilian county corrections employee, any call, volunteer,
26 or regular firefighter, civilian fire department employee, rescue or ambulance worker, including
27 ambulance service, emergency medical personnel, first responder service, and volunteer personnel,
28 telecommunicators, and local dispatchers, including any retired emergency response or public safety
29 workersasdefinedinthissection.

2 required by callers and walk-in customers for public safety services and dispatches the appropriate
3 police, fire, ambulance, or other units to provide needed emergency services at the state, city, town,
4 orprivateemergencyserviceslevel.
5 V. "Telecommunicator" means an employee of the department of safety, division of
6 emergency services and communications who is responsible for receiving at the public safety
7 answering point telephone calls made to E911 and transferring or relaying such calls to public or
8 privatesafetyagencies.
9 VI. "Certifiedpublicsafetycomfortdogteam"meansatrainedcertifiedpublicsafetycomfort
10 dog and its handler, working collaboratively to provide support, comfort, and crisis response to
11 individuals, groups, and communities in various settings. The certified public safety comfort dog
12 teamshallundergospecializedtrainingtodevelopskillsinempathy,activelistening,andnon-verbal
13 communication, enabling them to effectively respond to the emotional, behavioral, physical,
14 cognitive,andrelationalneedsofthosetheyencounter.
15 VII. "Certifiedpublic safetycomfort dogtrainer" meansanypersonwho isemployed totrain
16 dogs for the use of a certified public safety comfort dog or is volunteering and training to raise dogs
17 for the use of certified public safety comfort dogs by emergency response or public safety workers, or
18 an individual trainer who helps an emergency response or public safety worker to train their
19 certifiedpublicsafetycomfortdog.

21 real property, or portion thereof, which is used or occupied, or is intended, arranged, or designed to

24 sidewalk, walkway, public building, and any other place or structure to which the general public is
25 regularly,normally,orcustomarilypermittedorinvited.

4 167-E:2 CertifiedPublicSafetyComfortDogTraining.
5 I. The initial training and certification testing shall be completed by the dog’s second
6 birthday. SuchinitialtrainingandcertificationshallbecomprisedofthefollowingAmericanKennel

8 (a) CanineGoodCitizen(CGC).
9 (b) AdvancedCanineGoodCitizen(CGCA).
10 (c) UrbanCanineGoodCitizen(CGCU).
11 II. Theongoingtrainingshallbecomprisedofthefollowing:
12 (a) A minimum of 1 hour per month of structured training. Training shall focus on
13 maintainingandreinforcingCGC,CGCA,andCGCUskills.
14 (b) Exposure to various emergencies or public safety environments, including
15 interactionswithfirstrespondersandthepublic.
16 III. Theemergencyresponseorpublicsafetyagencyemployingorowningthecertifiedpublic

20 (c) Progressandanycorrectiveactionsneeded.
21 (d) Certificatecompletionandrenewaldates.
22 167-E:3 CertifiedPublicSafetyComfortDogHandlerTraining.
23 I. Each certified public safety comfort dog handler working with the certified public safety
24 comfort dog shall complete training with his or her assigned certified public safety comfort dog.
25 Such training and certificate testing shall be comprised of the following American Kennel Club
26 certificates,oranequivalentcertificatefromanothernationallyrecognizedorganization:
27 (a) CanineGoodCitizen(CGC).
28 (b) AdvancedCanineGoodCitizen(CGCA).
29 (c) UrbanCanineGoodCitizen(CGCU).
30 II. The following training shall also be required of certified public safety comfort dog
31 handlers:
32 (a) Training on recognizing and responding to individuals experiencing mental health
33 crises.
34 (b) Education on crisis intervention techniques for emergency response or public safety
35 workers,includingCITtraining.
36 (c) Trainingonprovidingbasicemergencycareandfirstaidforcaninepartners.

12 167-E:4 Animal Duties. The official duties performed by a certified public safety comfort dog
13 shall be directly related to the handler's role as an emergency response or public safety worker.
14 Official duties may include but are not limited to: critical incident stress management, survivor
15 advocacy and support, school support, crisis response, community mental health support, and
16 communityrelations.
17 167-E:5 CertifiedPublicSafetyComfortDogLicensing,RabiesCertificateandInsurance.
18 I. The owner or employer of the certified public safety comfort dog shall comply with all
19 state and local ordinances regarding licensing and rabies certification under RSA 466:1 and RSA
20 466:1-a. The owner or employer of the certified public safety comfort dog shall also fully insure the
21 dogforthedurationofitsservicecareer.

23 providedtoserviceanimalsandsearchandrescuedogsunderRSA167-D.

25 I. Each certified public safety comfort dog team shall complete all training requirements
26 outlined within RSA 167-E:2 and RSA 167-E:3 in order to be certified in the state of New
27 Hampshire.
28 II. Each certified public safety comfort dog shall be retested with his or her assigned
29 certified public safety comfort dog every 3 years. Such retesting shall be comprised of the following

31 organization:

33 (b) AdvancedCanineGoodCitizen(CGCA).
