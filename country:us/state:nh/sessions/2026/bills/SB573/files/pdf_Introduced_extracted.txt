4 FACILITYCOMFORTDOGCERTIFICATION
5 167-E:1 Definitions. Inthischapter:
6 I. "Facility comfort dog" means a specifically trained dog that provides emotional support,
7 comfort, and crisis response to individuals, groups, and communities in various settings, such as
8 hospitals, nursing homes, schools, disaster relief areas, emergency response/public safety agencies
9 and other places where people may be experiencing stress, trauma, or crisis. Facility comfort dogs
10 are trained to interact gently and empathetically with people, offering unconditional support and
11 non-judgmental companionship to help alleviate anxiety, reduce stress, improve mood, and enhance
12 overall well-being and resilience. Facility comfort dogs are not considered service dogs or emotional
13 supportanimals.
14 II. "Facilitycomfort doghandler" means anemergencyresponseor publicsafetyworker who
15 is responsible for the care, training, and supervision of a facility comfort dog during its interactions
16 withindividuals,groups,andcommunitiesinneedofemotionalsupportorcrisisresponse. Afacility
17 comfort dog handler shall ensure that it behaves appropriately, follows commands, and maintains a
18 calm and comforting presence in various environments. A facility comfort dog handlers shall be
19 trained to understand canine behavior, communication, and emotional support techniques, allowing
20 them to effectively guide the facility comfort dog's interactions with individuals, groups, or
21 communitiesindistressorcrisissituations.
22 III. "Emergency response or public safety worker" means any law enforcement officer
23 certified under RSA 106-L, certified county corrections officer, sheriff or deputy sheriff, state police
24 officer, civilian law enforcement employee, civilian county corrections employee, any call, volunteer,
25 or regular firefighter, civilian fire department employee, rescue or ambulance worker, including
26 ambulance service, emergency medical personnel, first responder service, and volunteer personnel,
27 telecommunicators, and local dispatchers, including any retired emergency response or public safety
28 workersasdefinedinthissection.
29 IV. "Local dispatcher" means a person who determines the location, status, and assistance
30 required by callers and walk-in customers for public safety services and dispatches the appropriate

1 police, fire, ambulance, or other units to provide needed emergency services at the state, city, town,
2 orprivateemergencyserviceslevel.
3 V. "Telecommunicator" means an employee of the department of safety, division of
4 emergency services and communications who is responsible for receiving at the public safety
5 answering point telephone calls made to E911 and transferring or relaying such calls to public or
6 privatesafetyagencies.
7 VI. "Facility comfort dog team" means a trained facility comfort dog and its handler,
8 working collaboratively to provide emotional support, comfort, and crisis response to individuals,
9 groups, and communities in various settings. The facility comfort dog team shall undergo
10 specialized training to develop skills in empathy, active listening, and non-verbal communication,
11 enabling them to effectively respond to the emotional, behavioral, physical, cognitive, and relational
12 needsofthosetheyencounter.
13 VII. "Facility comfort dog trainer" means any person who is employed to train dogs for the
14 use of a facility comfort dog or is volunteering and training to raise dogs for the use of facility
15 comfort dogs by emergency response or public safety worker, or an individual trainer who helps an
16 emergencyresponseorpublicsafetyworkertotraintheirfacilitycomfortdog.
17 VIII. "Housing accommodation" means any publicly assisted housing accommodation or any
18 real property, or portion thereof, which is used or occupied, or is intended, arranged, or designed to
19 beusedoroccupied,asthehome,residence,orsleepingplaceofoneormorepersons.
20 IX. "Public facility" means any place of public accommodation and any street, highway,
21 sidewalk, walkway, public building, and any other place or structure to which the general public is
22 regularly,normally,orcustomarilypermittedorinvited.
23 X. "Place of public accommodation" includes any tavern roadhouse, hotel, motel, or trailer
24 camp, whether for entertainment of transient guests or accommodation of those seeking health,
25 recreation, or rest; any producer, manufacturer, wholesaler, distributor, retail shop, store
26 establishment, or concession dealing with goods or services of any kind; any restaurant, eating
27 house,orplacewherefoodissoldforconsumptiononthepremises;anyplacemaintainedforthesale
28 of ice cream, ice, and fruit preparations or their derivatives, soda water or confections, or where any
29 beverages of any kind are retailed for consumption on the premises; any garage; any public
30 conveyance operated on land or water, or in the air, or any stations and terminals thereof; any
31 bathhouse, boardwalk, or seashore accommodation; any auditorium, meeting place, or hall; any
32 theater,motionpicturehouse,musichall,roofgarden,skatingrink,swimmingpool,amusementand
33 recreation park, fair, bowling alley, gymnasium, shooting gallery, billiard and pool parlor, or any
34 otherplaceofamusement;anycomfortstation;anydispensary,clinic,orhospital;anypubliclibrary;
35 any kindergarten, primary and secondary school, trade or business school, high school, academy,
36 college and university, or any educational institution under the supervision of the state board of
37 education,orthecommissionerofeducationofthestateofNewHampshire.

1 167-E:2 FacilityComfortDogTraining.
2 I. The initial training and certification shall be completed by the dog’s second birthday.

4 certifications:
5 (a) CanineGoodCitizen(CGC).

7 (c) UrbanCanineGoodCitizen(CGCU).

18 (d) Certificationcompletionandrenewaldates.

20 I. Each facility comfort dog handler working with the facility comfort dog shall complete
21 training with his or her assigned facility comfort dog. Such training and certification shall be
22 comprisedofthefollowingAmericanKennelClubcertifications:
23 (a) CanineGoodCitizen(CGC).
24 (b) AdvancedCanineGoodCitizen(CGCA).
25 (c) UrbanCanineGoodCitizen(CGCU).
26 II. Thefollowingtrainingshallalsoberequiredoffacilitycomfortdoghandlers:
27 (a) Training on recognizing and responding to individuals experiencing mental health
28 crises.
29 (b) Education on crisis intervention techniques for first emergency response or public
30 safetyworkers,includingCITtraining.
31 (c) Trainingonprovidingbasicemergencycareandfirstaidforcaninepartners.
32 III. Theongoingtrainingshallbecomprisedofthefollowing:
33 (a) A minimum of one hour per month of structured training with the facility comfort
34 dog. Training should focus on reinforcing AKC certification skills, handling techniques, and
35 scenario-basedpublicinteractions.
36 (b) Regularexposuretoemergency/publicsafetyenvironmentstomaintainproficiency.

7 167-E:4 Animal Duties. The official duties performed by a facility comfort dog shall be directly
8 related to the handler's roll as an emergency response or public safety worker. Official duties may
9 include but are not limited to: critical incident stress management, survivor advocacy and support,
10 schoolsupport,crisisresponse,communitymentalhealthsupport,communityrelations.
11 167-E:5 FacilityComfortDogLicensing,RabiesCertificateandInsurance.
12 I. The owner of the facility comfort dog shall comply with all state and local ordinances
13 regarding licensing and rabies certification under RSA 466:1 and RSA 466:1-a. The owner of the
14 facilitycomfortdogshallalsofullyinsurethedogforthedurationofitsservicecareer.
15 II. Nothing in this section is intended to inhibit, interfere or impede with the protections
16 providedtoserviceanimalsandsearchandrescuedogsunderRSA167-D.
17 167-E:6 FacilityComfortDogTeamCertification.
18 I. Each facility comfort dog team shall complete all training requirements outlined within
19 the facility comfort dog training under RSA 167-E:2, and facility comfort dog handler training under
20 RSA167-E:3inordertobecertifiedinthestateofNewHampshire.
21 II. Each facility comfort dog team shall complete the following certification exams every 3
22 years,fromthedateoftheirinitialcertification,toremaincertified:
23 (a) CanineGoodCitizen(CGC).
24 (b) AdvancedCanineGoodCitizen(CGCA).
25 (c) UrbanCanineGoodCitizen(CGCU).
26 III. The emergency services or public safety agency owning or employing the facility comfort
27 dogshallmaintainalltrainingandcertificationrecords,including:
28 (a) Initialtherapydogcertificationdocumentation.
29 (b) Recertificationdatesandresults.
30 (c) Anyongoingtrainingorcorrectiveactionrecords.
31 IV. All facility comfort dog teams working in the state of New Hampshire shall have one
32 year from the effective date of this chapter to meet certification and recertification requirements
33 outlinedtherein.
34 167-E:7 Facility Comfort Dog May Accompany. A facility comfort dog may accompany their
35 handler into any public facility, housing accommodation, or place of public accommodation to which
36 the general public is invited, while they are in the course of performing their official duties, and/or,
37 whileinthecourseof,ortravelingtoorfromthelocationof,theirofficialduties.

1 167-E:8 ProhibitedActs.
2 I. It is unlawful for a person, directly or indirectly, either to prohibit, hinder, or interfere
3 withafacilitycomfortdogteam,whileinthecourseofperformingtheirofficialduties.
4 II. It isunlawful for anypersontorepresent, and/or fit,adogwithacollar, leash, vest, sign,
5 or harness of any type which represents that the dog is a facility comfort dog if the dog is not a

7 167-E:9 FacilityComfortDogCare.
8 I. A facility comfort dog shall not be fed a raw diet in order to minimize food borne diseases

12 temperature sensor shall be placed in any vehicle in which the facility comfort dog will be left
13 unattended.
14 III. No prong or electronic collars or head halters shall be used for American Kennel Club
15 testingandnodogmaywearprongorelectroniccollarsorheadhalterswhilebeingdeployed.
