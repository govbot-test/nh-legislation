BeitEnactedbytheSenateandHouseofRepresentativesinGeneralCourtconvened:
1 1 TransportationofBeveragesandWine;LiquorIncluded. AmendRSA179:15,III-IVtoreadas
2 follows:
3 III.(a) Notwithstanding the provisions of paragraph II, an on-premises licensee may
4 purchase a restaurant delivery license from the commission for $250. Such license shall expire one
5 yearafterthedateofissueandmayberenewedbyanon-premiseslicenseefor$250eachyear.
6 (b) An on-premises licensee may only deliver or sell for take-out, products such
7 licenseeisauthorizedtosell.
8 [(b)] (c) An on-premises licensee in possession of a restaurant delivery license shall
9 [only] be permitted to transport beverages and wines for delivery to consumers subject to the
10 followingrequirements:
11 (1) All deliveries of beverages, liquors, and wines shall be accompanied by food
12 preparedbytherestaurantdeliverylicenseeandorderedbytheconsumer;
13 (2) All deliveries of beverages, liquors, and wine conducted pursuant to this section
14 shall be undertaken during the on-premises licensee's hours of operation and shall be delivered only
15 toareaswherethesaleofalcoholicbeveragesispermitted;
16 (3) All deliveries of beverages, liquors, and wine shall be solely for the personal
17 consumptionoftheconsumerandnotforresale;
18 (4) All beverages and wines transported pursuant to RSA 179:15, II shall be
19 transportedintheiroriginal,manufactured,sealedcontainers;
20 (5) All drinks containing liquor transported pursuant to RSA 179:15, II shall
21 be transported in a sealed container provided by the restaurant delivery license. The
22 container shall clearly identify the licensee from which the alcoholic beverage was
23 purchased. The sealed container shall use a closure that clearly demonstrates when the
24 containerhasbeenopened.
25 (6) Any individual engaged in the delivery of beverages and wines pursuant to this
26 section shall be an employee who regularly receives a W-2 from the on-premises licensee and is at
27 least21yearsofage;and
28 [(6)] (7) During deliveries conducted under this section, the person engaged in
29 making the delivery shall acquire a signed receipt from the consumer. Consumers who appear
30 visibly intoxicated or who a reasonable and prudent person would know are intoxicated, who do not
SB524 -ASINTRODUCED
-Page2-
1 produce identification verifying the consumer's age, or who fail to sign a receipt shall not be entitled
2 tohisorherdeliveryofbeverages,liquors,orwine.
3 [(c)] (d) No holder of a restaurant delivery license or on-premises license shall deliver
4 any alcoholic beverage to any college, university, or school, whether public or private, located within
5 the state. No holder of a restaurant delivery license or on-premises license shall deliver any
6 alcoholicbeveragetoanypubliclibrary,publicplayground,orpublicpark.
7 IV. A person holding an on-premises license engaged in take-out services may include
8 beverages, liquors, and wine with meals sold to a consumer for consumption by the consumer and
9 not for resale. The requirements set forth in subparagraph III(b) shall apply to any take-out meal
10 soldwithbeverages,liquors,orwine.
11 2 EffectiveDate. Thisactshalltakeeffect60daysafteritspassage.
