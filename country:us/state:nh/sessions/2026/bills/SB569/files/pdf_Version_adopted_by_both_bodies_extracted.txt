COMMITTEE: ExecutiveDepartmentsandAdministration
─────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
AMENDEDANALYSIS
Thisbillrequireshearingofficersatthedepartmentoflabortobegraduatesofanaccreditedlaw
school and directs the labor commissioner to give a hiring preference to licensed attorneys who have
workers'compensationexperienceorwhohaveadministrativeadjudicationexperience.

ANACT relativetothequalificationsforhearingsofficerswithinthedepartmentoflabor.
BeitEnactedbytheSenateandHouseofRepresentativesinGeneralCourtconvened:
1 1 HearingOfficers. AmendRSA281-A:42-btoreadasfollows:
2 281-A:42-b Hearing Officers. The commissioner shall appoint as many individuals as necessary
3 to carry out the department's responsibilities under this chapter. Such individuals [shall have
4 experience in workers' compensation and] shall hear workers' compensation cases before the
5 commissioner. The commissioner shall set forth the job qualification necessary to [insure] ensure
6 that each hearing officer is qualified to hear workers' compensation cases. Individuals employed
7 ashearingofficersshallhavegraduatedfromanaccreditedlawschool. Thecommissioner
8 shall give a hiring preference to licensed attorneys who have workers' compensation
9 experience or who have administrative adjudication experience. The hearing officer salary
10 shall be commensurate with the responsibilities and experience required. The commissioner shall,
11 by rules adopted under RSA 541-A, strengthen the reporting structure and the role of a hearing
12 officer; develop a code of ethics for hearings and hearing officers; develop and require at least 15
13 hours of continuing education on an annual basis for hearing officers; and require a minimum of an
14 additional15hoursofannualtrainingandbriefingwiththeattorneygeneral'sstaff.
15 2 EffectiveDate. Thisactshalltakeeffect60daysafteritspassage.
