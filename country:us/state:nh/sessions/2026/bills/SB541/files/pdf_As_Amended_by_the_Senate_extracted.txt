541-FN-A
ANACT relativetocapitalappropriationsforregionaldrinkingwaterinfrastructure.
SPONSORS: Sen. Abbas, Dist 22; Sen. McGough, Dist 11; Sen. Birdsell, Dist 19; Sen. Avard,
Dist 12; Sen. Carson, Dist 14; Rep. DeSimone, Rock. 18; Rep. Donnelly, Rock. 25;
Rep.Harb,Rock.20;Rep.Nelson,Rock.13;Rep.Sweeney,Rock.25
COMMITTEE: Finance
─────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
AMENDEDANALYSIS
Thisbill:
I. Makes an appropriation to the department of environmental services to the Pillsbury Lake
Village District for the purpose of addressing loan costs associated with certain water system
upgrades.
II. Requires a share of moneys appropriated to the department of environmental services in
2020tobeusedfortheSouthernNewHampshireRegionalWaterInterconnectionProject.

BeitEnactedbytheSenateandHouseofRepresentativesinGeneralCourtconvened:
1 1 Project Amended; Department of Environmental Services. Amend 2020, 30:6 to read as
2 follows:
3 30:6 Capital Appropriation; Department of Environmental Services. The sum of $50,000,000 is
4 hereby appropriated to the department of environmental services for capital expenditures for the
5 remediation of per- and polyfluoroalkyl substances in the state’s drinking water sources and
6 wastewater residuals, which expenditures may include loans to public or private entities on such
7 terms and conditions as the department of environmental services shall determine. Of the
8 amounts appropriated, $5,000,000 shall be a grant and used for the Southern New
9 Hampshire Regional Water Interconnection Project, Phase 2B. To provide funds for the
10 appropriation made in this section, the state treasurer is hereby authorized to borrow upon the
11 credit of the state not exceeding the sum of $50,000,000 and for said purpose may issue bonds and
12 notes in the name of and on behalf of the state of New Hampshire in accordance with RSA 6-13-e.
13 NotwithstandingtheprovisionsofRSA9:18,theappropriationmadeinthissectionshallnotlapse.
14 2 Appropriation; Department of Environmental Services; Pipe, Water Filtration and Well
15 Upgrades;PillsburyLakeVillageDistrict. Amend2025,141:380toreadasfollows:
16 141:380 Appropriation;DepartmentofEnvironmentalServices;Pipe,WaterFiltration,andWell
17 Upgrades; Pillsbury Lake Village District. The sum of $325,000 for the biennium ending June 30,
18 2027, is appropriated to the department of environmental services to the Pillsbury Lake Village
19 Districtforthepurposeofaddressing[loan]costsassociatedwiththefollowingupgrades:
20 I. Piping Upgrades: $155,000 to cover a loan used for upgrading the pipes under Concord
21 DriveinWebster.
22 II. Water Filtration Upgrade: $105,000 to cover a loan for upgrading the water filtration
23 system.
24 III. [New Well Development] Water System Improvements: $65,000 to cover [a loan for
25 digging a new well, known as the Franklin-Pierce well, which requires less processing and will
26 reduce overall costs] water system improvements as agreed upon by both the department of
27 environmentalservicesandPillsburyLakeVillageDistrict.
28 The governor is authorized to draw a warrant for said sum out of any money in the treasury not
29 otherwiseappropriated.
30 3 EffectiveDate. ThisactshalltakeeffectJuly1,2026.

Section 2:
Section one of this bill directs that $5,000,000 of the existing $50,000,000 capital appropriation
made to the Department of Environmental Services in 2020 for the remediation of per- and
polyfluoroalkyl substances in the state’s drinking water sources and wastewater residuals be
usedas agrant fortheSouthernNew HampshireRegional WaterInterconnectionProject, Phase
2B. Thisfundingwillincreaselocalrevenueandexpendituresbyupto$5,000,000.

Section 3:
Sectiontwoof this bill revises the purpose of $65,000of theappropriationenacted inLaws 2025,
141:380, from new well development to water system improvements, as agreed upon by both the
Department of Environmental Services and the Pillsbury Lake Village District. This section
further allows the Department to pay the District directly versus the District needing to take a
loanoutpriortosubmittingforreimbursementfromtheDepartment. Thischangemaydecrease
coststotheDistrictrelatedtoacquiringaloan.
