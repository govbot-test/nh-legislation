ANACT relativetoplay-basedcurriculumandphysicaleducationcurriculum.
SPONSORS: Sen. Sullivan, Dist 18; Sen. Pearl, Dist 17; Sen. Carson, Dist 14; Sen. Avard, Dist
12; Sen. Birdsell, Dist 19; Sen. Murphy, Dist 16; Rep. Freeman, Belk. 8; Rep.
Peeples,Hills.14;Rep.Rice,Hills.38
COMMITTEE: Education
─────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
AMENDEDANALYSIS
Thisbill:
I. Extendsplay-basedcurriculumfromkindergartenuntilthirdgrade.
II. Requiresrecessforallstudentsfromkindergartenuntiltheeighthgradeasapartofphysical
educationcurriculum.
III. Defines recess as physical activity that promotes fitness and well-being and prohibits the
useofelectronicdevicesduringrecess.
IV. Specifiesthatelementaryandmiddleschoolsmaycountrecessasinstructionaltime.
V. Limits withholding pupils from recess unless a pupil's participation poses a physical safety
risk.

ANACT relativetoplay-basedcurriculumandphysicaleducationcurriculum.
BeitEnactedbytheSenateandHouseofRepresentativesinGeneralCourtconvened:
1 1 ExpansionofPlay-BasedCurriculum. AmendRSA193-E:2-a,II-atoreadasfollows:
2 II-a. Instruction in support of standards for kindergarten [standards] through third
3 grade shall be engaging and shall foster children's development and learning in all domains
4 including physical, social, cognitive, and language. Educators shall create a learning environment
5 that facilitates high quality, child-directed experiences based upon early childhood best teaching
6 practices and play-based learning that comprise movement, creative expression, exploration,
7 socialization, and music. Educators shall develop literacy through guided reading and shall provide
8 unstructuredtimeforthediscoveryofeachchild'sindividualtalents,abilities,andneeds.
9 2 New Paragraph; Recess Physical Education Requirement. Amend RSA 189:10 by inserting
10 afterparagraphIIIthefollowingnewparagraph:
11 IV. The school board shall ensure that recess is included in the physical education
12 curriculum for all students in grades kindergarten through eighth grade, with the exception of
13 schools where eighth grade is a part of the high school. Elementary schools and middle schools, as
14 described in RSA 189:25, may count recess as instructional time for pupils in kindergarten through
15 eighthgrade.
16 (a) For the purposes of this paragraph, recess shall be defined as physical activity that
17 promotes fitness and well-being which shall include unstructured play outside, unless weather
18 conditionsnegativelyimpactthesafetyofphysicalactivitytakingplaceoutside.
19 (b) For the purposes of this paragraph, recess shall not include any time considered
20 instructionaltimeforaphysicaleducationclass.
21 (c) For the purposes of this paragraph, recess shall not involve the use of electronic
22 devices by students, including but not limited to cell phones, laptops, tablets, and video game
23 consolesordevices.
24 (d) A pupil shall not be denied recess by amember of the school’s staff unless the pupil’s
25 participationposesanimmediatethreattothephysicalsafetyofthepupilortothephysicalsafetyof
26 one or more of the pupil’s peers. If a pupil’s recess period is denied for safety concerns, school staff
27 shall make all reasonable efforts to resolve such threats and minimize exclusion from recess to the
28 greatestextentpossible.
29 3 EffectiveDate. Thisactshalltakeeffect60daysafteritspassage.
