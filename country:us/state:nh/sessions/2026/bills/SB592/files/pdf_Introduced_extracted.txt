ANACT enabling regional, conservation, and energy resource planning for habitat
strongholdsandwildlifecorridors.
BeitEnactedbytheSenateandHouseofRepresentativesinGeneralCourtconvened:
1 1 Conservation Commission; Habitat Strongholds and Wildlife Corridors Included. Amend RSA
2 36-A:2toreadasfollows:
3 36-A:2 Conservation Commission. A city or town which accepts the provisions of this chapter
4 may establish a conservation commission, hereinafter called the commission, for the proper
5 utilization and protection of the natural resources and for the protection of watershed resources of
6 said city or town. Such commission shall conduct researches into its local land and water areas and
7 shall seek to coordinate the activities of unofficial bodies organized for similar purposes, and may
8 advertise, prepare, print and distribute books, maps, charts, plans and pamphlets which in its
9 judgment it deems necessary for its work. It shall keep an index of all open space and natural,
10 aesthetic or ecological areas, including habitat strongholds as defined in RSA 207:1, XIII-a
11 andwildlife corridorsasdefinedinRSA207:1,XXXVI,withinthecityortown,asthecasemay
12 be, with the plan of obtaining information pertinent to proper utilization of such areas, including
13 lands owned by the state or lands owned by a town or city. It shall keep an index of all marshlands,
14 swamps and all other wet lands in a like manner, and may recommend to the city council or
15 selectmen or to the department of natural and cultural resources a program for the protection,
16 developmentorbetterutilizationofallsuchareas. Itshallkeepaccuraterecordsofitsmeetingsand
17 actions and shall file an annual report which shall be printed in the annual town or municipal
18 report. The commission may appoint such clerks and other employees or subcommittees as it may
19 fromtimetotimerequire.
20 2 Purposes; Habitat Strongholds and Wildlife Corridors Included. Amend RSA 36:45 to read as
21 follows:
22 36:45 Purposes. The purpose of this subdivision shall be to enable municipalities and counties
23 to join in the formation of regional planning commissions whose duty it shall be to prepare a
24 coordinated plan for the development of a region, taking into account present and future needs with
25 a view toward encouraging the most appropriate use of land, such as for agriculture, forestry,
26 conservation, including habitat strongholds as defined in RSA 207:1, XIII-a and wildlife
27 corridors as defined in RSA 207:1, XXXVI, industry, commerce, and housing; the facilitation of
28 transportation and communication; the proper and economic location of public utilities and services;
29 thedevelopmentofadequaterecreationalareas;thepromotionofgoodcivicdesign;andthewiseand
30 efficient expenditure of public funds. The aforesaid plan shall be made in order to promote the
SB592 -ASINTRODUCED
-Page2-
1 health, safety, morals and general welfare of the region and its inhabitants. To promote these
2 purposes the office of planning and development shall delineate planning regions for the state so
3 thateachmunicipalityofthestatewillfallwithinadelineatedregionandshallhavetheopportunity
4 of forming or joining the regional planning commission for that planning region. In determining
5 these regions the office shall consider such factors as community of interest and homogeneity,
6 existing metropolitan and regional planning agencies, patterns of communication and
7 transportation, geographic features and natural boundaries, extent of urban development, relevancy
8 of theregion for provisionof governmental services and functions and its use for administering state
9 and federal programs, the existence of physical, social and economic problems of a regional
10 character, and other related characteristics. To accommodate changing conditions, the office may
11 adjust the boundaries of the planning regions, after consultation with the respective regional
12 planningcommissions.
13 3 Regional Planning Commission; Powers and Duties; Habitat Strongholds and Wildlife
14 CorridorsIncluded. AmendRSA36:47,Itoreadasfollows:
15 I. A regional planning commission's powers shall be advisory, and shall generally pertain to
16 thedevelopment of theregionwithinits jurisdictionas awhole. Nothinginthis subdivisionshall be
17 deemed to reduce or limit any of the powers, duties or obligations of planning boards in individual
18 municipalities. The area of jurisdiction of a regional planning commission shall include the areas of
19 the respective municipalities within the delineated planning region. It shall be the duty of a
20 regional planning commission to prepare a comprehensive master plan for the development of the
21 region within its jurisdiction, including the commission's recommendations, among other things, for
22 the use and conservation of land within the region; for the general location, extent, type of use,
23 and character of highways, major streets, intersections, parking lots, railroads, aircraft landing
24 areas, waterways and bridges, and other means of transportation, communication, and other
25 purposes; for the development, extent, and general location of parks, playgrounds, shore front
26 developments, parkways, and other public reservations and recreation areas, land conservation
27 areas, including habitat strongholds as defined in RSA 207:1, XIII-a and wildlife corridors
28 as defined in RSA 207:1, XXXVI; for the location, type, and character of public buildings, schools,
29 community centers, and other public property; and for the improvement, redevelopment,
30 rehabilitation, or conservation of residential, business, industrial and other areas; including the
31 development of programs for the modernization and coordination of buildings, housing, zoning and
32 subdivision regulations of municipalities and their enforcement on a coordinated and unified basis.
33 A regional planning commission may authorize its employees or consultants to render assistance on
34 local planning problems to any municipality or county which is not a member of said regional
35 planning commission. The cost of such assistance shall be paid entirely by the municipality or
36 county to which the service is rendered or partly by said municipality or county and partly by any
37 gift, grant, or contribution which may be available for such work or by combination thereof. Said

1 commission shall keep a strict account of the cost of such assistance and shall provide such
2 municipalityorcountywithanitemizedstatement.
3 4 New Paragraph; Development of Regional Impact; Habitat Strongholds and Wildlife Corridors
4 Included. AmendRSA36:55byinsertingafterparagraphVIthefollowingnewparagraph:
5 VII. Conservation,includinghabitatstrongholdsasdefinedinRSA207:1,XIII-aandwildlife
6 corridorsasdefinedinRSA207:1,XXXVI.
7 5 New Paragraph; Executive Director Fish and Game Commission; Consultations Regarding
8 HabitatStrongholdsand WildlifeCorridors. AmendRSA206:10byinsertingafter paragraphIVthe
9 followingnewparagraph:
10 V. Theexecutivedirectorofthefishandgamecommissionshall:
11 (a) Consult with regional planning commissions, conservation commissions, and the
12 department of transportation concerning habitat strongholds as defined in RSA 207:1, XIII-a and
13 wildlifecorridorsasdefinedinRSA207:1,XXXVI.
14 (b) Consult with the department of energy and the department of environmental
15 services onpotential designationof approved transmissioncorridors and large-scale solar projects as
16 habitat strongholds as defined in RSA 243;3, III-a and wildlife corridors as defined in RSA 207:1,
17 XXXVI,subjecttoagreementofthepropertyowners.
