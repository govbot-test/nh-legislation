STATEOFNEWHAMPSHIRE
IntheYearofOurLordTwoThousandTwenty-Six
ANACT creating a Medicaid methodology working group within the department of health
andhumanservices.
BeitEnactedbytheSenateandHouseofRepresentativesinGeneralCourtconvened:
1 1 Department of Health and Human Services; Medicaid Methodology Working Group
2 Established.
3 I. Thedepartmentofhealthandhumanservicesshallconveneaworkinggroupforthestudy
4 of nursing home rate Medicaid methodology. The working group, which shall be exempt from RSA
5 14:49,shallconsistofthefollowingmembers:
6 (a) Three members of thehouse of representatives, 2of whom shall befrom the majority
7 party and one of whom shall be from the minority party, appointed by the speaker of the house of
8 representatives;
9 (b) Amemberofthesenate,appointedbythepresidentofthesenate;
10 (c) Thecommissionerofthedepartmentofhealthandhumanservices,ordesignee;
11 (d) A member of the New Hampshire Association of Counties, appointed by that
12 organization;
13 (e) A member of the New Hampshire Health Care Association, appointed by that
14 organization;
15 (f) A member of the New Hampshire Hospital Association, appointed by that
16 organization;
17 (g) A certified public accountant specializing in nursing home cost reporting, appointed
18 bythegovernor;and
19 (h) An administrator at a private or publicly funded nursing home, appointed by the
20 governor.
21 II. The working group shall be convened by the commissioner of department of health and
22 human services, or their designee, and shall be subject to RSA 91-A. The working group shall make
23 recommendations concerning the nursing home reimbursement methodology, with a focus on the
24 followingconsiderations:
25 (a) Accesstonursinghomecareandtheexistenceofwaitinglists;
26 (b) Barrierstohospitaldischargetonursinghomes;
27 (c) Whether the acuity or direct care needs of nursing home residents are properly
28 capturedbyreimbursement;
29 (d) Capitalreimbursementinlightofagingnursinghomeinfrastructure;
SB663-FN-A-VERSIONADOPTEDBYBOTHBODIES
-Page2-
1 (e) Therelationshipbetweenworkforceneedsofnursinghomesandreimbursement;
2 (f) Theoverallsolvencyofthenursinghomesector;and
3 (g) The fairest means of addressing changes of ownership within reimbursement in a
4 mannerthatprotectsMedicaidprogramintegrity.
5 III. No later than December 31, 2026, the commissioner of the department of health and
6 human services, or their designee, shall submit to the speaker of the house of representatives, the
7 president of the senate, the house clerk, the senate clerk, the governor, the state library, and the
8 county-state finance commission any proposed changes to state law or regulations the department,
9 inconsultationwiththeworkinggroup,believesisnecessaryifthedepartmentintendstomodifythe
10 nursinghomeMedicaidratemethodology.
11 2 EffectiveDate. Thisactshalltakeeffectuponitspassage.
