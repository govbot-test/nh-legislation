452
ANACT enabling health care providers to offer certain complementary and alternative
healthcareservices.
SPONSORS: Sen.Ward,Dist8;Sen.Long,Dist20;Sen.Avard,Dist12;Sen.Rochefort,Dist1
COMMITTEE: HealthandHumanServices
─────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
ANALYSIS
This bill enables an individual to offer certain complementary and alternative health care
services to patients notwithstanding state licensing requirements, provided that the individual does
not engage in certain conduct, and the individual discloses their qualifications and obtains informed
consentfromthepatientforsuchservices.

BeitEnactedbytheSenateandHouseofRepresentativesinGeneralCourtconvened:
1 1 New Chapter; Complementary and Alternative Health Care Practice Act. Amend RSA by
2 insertingafterchapter310-Cthefollowingnewchapter:
3 CHAPTER310-D
4 COMPLEMENTARYANDALTERNATIVEHEALTHCAREPRACTICEACT
5 310-D:1 Short Title. This chapter may be cited as the "Complementary and Alternative Health
6 CarePracticeAct".
7 310-D:2 Definition of Complementary and Alternative Health Care Services. "Complementary
8 and alternative health care services" means health care and healing methods, modalities,
9 treatments, procedures, or protocols that have not been generally adopted by a profession, or that
10 arenotgenerallyconsideredtobewithintheprevailingstandardsofcareofaprofession.
11 310-D:3 Licensing Exemption. Notwithstanding New Hampshire licensing laws related to
12 health care occupations and services, a person who provides complementary and alternative health
13 care services and who is not licensed by the state of New Hampshire to practice a health care
14 occupation shall not violate any New Hampshire licensing law relating to health care occupations
15 andservicesunlessthatindividual:
16 I. EngagesinanactivityprohibitedunderRSA310-D:4;or
17 II. FailstofulfillthedutiessetforthinRSA310-D:5.
18 310-D:4 Prohibited Acts. A person providing complementary and alternative health care
19 servicesshallnot:
20 I. Performsurgeryonanindividual.
21 II. Administerx-rayradiationtoanindividual.
22 III. Setfracturesonanindividual.
23 IV. Prescribeordispenseprescriptiondrugsorcontrolledsubstancestoanindividual.
24 V. Directlymanipulatethejointsorspineofanindividual.
25 VI. Make a recommendation to discontinue current medical treatment prescribed by a
26 licensedhealthcarepractitioner.
27 VII. Hold himself or herself out as or imply that he or she is licensed by the state of New
28 Hampshireasahealthcareproviderifheorsheisnot.

1 310-D:5 Disclosures. Except for persons acting pursuant to the direction of licensed health care
2 facilities or licensed health care providers while working within the scope of their employment or
3 direction,acomplementaryandalternativehealthcarepractitionershalldoallofthefollowing:
4 I. Provide to a client prior to rendering services a client information document, either in
5 writing in plain language that the client understands or, if the client cannot read, orally in a
6 languagetheclientunderstands,containingthefollowing:
7 (a) The name, complementary and alternative health care title, business address, and
8 telephonenumberofthepersonprovidingthecomplementaryandalternativehealthcareservices.
9 (b) A statement that the person providing complementary and alternative health care
10 servicesisnotahealthcarepractitionerlicensedbythestateofNewHampshire.
11 (c) A statement describing the nature of the complementary and alternative health care
12 servicesgenerallyprovided.
13 (d) Thedegrees,education,training,experience,orotherqualifications,ifany,regarding
14 the complementary and alternative health care services to be provided held by the person providing
15 thecomplementaryandalternativehealthcareservice.
16 II. Post a copyof theclient information document in aprominent locationin theoffice of the
17 unlicensedcomplementaryandalternativehealthcarepractitioner.
18 III. Obtain a written acknowledgment from a client, stating that the client has been
19 provided with a copy of the information document described in paragraph I. The client shall be
20 provided with a copy of the written acknowledgment, which shall be maintained for 2 years by the
21 complementary and alternative health care practitioner providing the complementary and
22 alternativehealthcareservice.
23 310-D:6 Civil Relief. Nothing in this chapter shall be construed to limit the right of any
24 person to seek relief for negligence or any other civil remedy against a person providing services
25 subjecttotherequirementsofthischapter.
26 310-D:7 Scope of Practice. This chapter shall not affect the scope of practice of a health care
27 professional licensed under this title or their right to practice complementary or alternative health
28 care. This chapter shall not be construed to prevent a person from providing complementary or
29 alternative health care services that would otherwise be exempt under this title. This chapter may
30 not be construed to require a person to be licensed to provide services who would otherwise be
31 exemptunderthistitle.
32 2 EffectiveDate. ThisactshalltakeeffectJanuary1,2027.
