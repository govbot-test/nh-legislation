A.Davis,Coos2;Rep.Ouellet,Coos3
COMMITTEE: Commerce
─────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
ANALYSIS
This bill promotes the use of New Hampshire-grown spruce-pine-fir lumber by specifying that
spruce-pine-fir (SPF) lumber shall include spruce-pine-fir-south (SPFS) lumber within the New
Hampshirebuildingcode.

STATEOFNEWHAMPSHIRE
IntheYearofOurLordTwoThousandTwenty-Six
ANACT relativetotheuseofspruce-pine-firlumber.
BeitEnactedbytheSenateandHouseofRepresentativesinGeneralCourtconvened:
1 1 StatementofFindingsandPurpose. Thegeneralcourtfindsthat:
2 I. The majority of structural timber harvested for building materials in New Hampshire
3 comes from Coos County and many areas of Coos County extend farther north than portions of
4 Canadawherelumberisgradedassprucepinefir(SPF).
5 II. TimbergradedasSPFfromCanadaisviewedasstrongeranddenserunderinternational
6 design standards in part because of cold climate growth conditions; however, New Hampshire's
7 northern climate, soils, and growing conditions are substantially similar, and produce the same
8 speciesofspruce,pine,andfirincludedintheSPFdesignation.
9 III. Architects and engineers typically specify "SPF" lumber in certain residential
10 construction documents. Because "SPF" is an international designation reserved to Canadian
11 graded wood, New Hampshire's native lumber graders cannot certify New Hampshire-grown spruce,
12 pine, and fir as SPF. Instead, the same species must be graded as spruce-pine-fir south (SPFS),
13 whichisviewedintheindustryasaseparate,slightlyweakerclassification.
14 IV. This technical distinction disadvantages New Hampshire sawmills, loggers, and the
15 forest products industry, even though New Hampshire-grown wood from the north country is of
16 equaltypeandquality.
17 V. It is therefore the policy of the state of New Hampshire to ensure that New Hampshire
18 families and New Hampshire builders can build New Hampshire homes with New Hampshire wood
19 byeliminatingthebarrierbetweenSPFandSPFSwithinthestatebuildingcode.
20 VI. The general court of New Hampshire finds that wood density should be a latitudinal
21 demarcationratherthananarbitrarydesignationofdensitybaseduponpoliticalborders. Viewedas
22 latitude, New Hampshire encompasses 42 degrees, 40 minutes north up to 45 degrees, 18 minutes.
23 Timber harvested to produce building lumber between the latitude of 43 degrees, 45 minutes, 18
24 seconds north to 45 degrees, 18 minutes north warrants the same density demarcation as Canadian
25 SPFunlessotherwisedesignatedbyalicensedarchitectorstructuralengineer.
26 2 New Section; Use of Spruce-Pine-Fir (SPF) and Spruce-Pine-Fir-South (SPFS). Amend RSA
27 155-Abyinsertingaftersection14thefollowingnewsection:
28 155-A:15 UseofSpruce-Pine-Fir(SPF)LumberandSpruce-Pine-Fir-South(SPFS)Lumber.
29 I. For purposes of the state building code, spruce-pine-fir south (SPFS) lumber harvested
30 and milled in New Hampshire and graded either by a grader permitted under RSA 426:18 or by an
SB503-VERSIONADOPTEDBYBOTHBODIES
-Page2-
1 American Lumber Standard Committee (ALSC) accredited agency shall be accepted wherever
2 spruce-pine-fir(SPF)isspecified.
3 II. Structural design and inspection shall use the published SPFS design values for the
4 gradeandsizeprovided.
5 III. Where a code evaluation report, product listing, or manufacturer's installation
6 instructions differentiate allowable loads or limitations by lumber species group, the allowable loads
7 corresponding to the installed species group shall be used. When SPFS lumber is installed, the
8 valuesforSPFSshallgovern. Thisrequirementshallapply,butnotbelimitedto,structural
9 connectors,hangers,fasteners,trussplates,adhesives,andproprietarysystems.
10 IV. Municipalities shall accept either a native lumber certificate pursuant to RSA 426:18 or
11 anALSCgradestampassufficientproofofgrade.
12 V. Nothing herein shall prevent a licensed architect or engineer from specifying higher
13 strength materials if required for safety and structural integrity of a structure. Licensed architects
14 and structural engineers shall also consider and shall provide alternative options of construction
15 utilizingSPFSlumber.
16 VI. ThissectionshallnotbeconstruedtoprohibittheuseofSPFlumbergradedbyanALSC
17 accreditedagency.
18 3 EffectiveDate. Thisactshalltakeeffect180daysafterpassage.
