STATEOFNEWHAMPSHIRE
IntheYearofOurLordTwoThousandTwenty-Six
ANACT relativetoexaminationrequirementsforlicensureasachiropractor.
BeitEnactedbytheSenateandHouseofRepresentativesinGeneralCourtconvened:
1 1 Chiropractic;ExaminationRequirements. AmendRSA316-A:17toreadasfollows:
2 316-A:17 National Examination; Examination Requirements. The board shall require that
3 applicants pass parts 1, 2, and 3 [and] of the written clinical competency examinations of the
4 national examination given by the National Board of Chiropractic Examiners if an applicant was
5 licensed after January 1, 1990[, and parts 1, 2, 3, and 4 and the written clinical competency
6 examinations of the national examination given by the National Board of Chiropractic Examiners if
7 the applicant was licensed or applied for a license after January 1, 1996]. Applicants shall pass
8 the ethics and jurisprudence examination given by the New Hampshire board of
9 chiropractic examiners. [Applicants having passed the national examinations shall be exempt
10 from taking a written examination and,] Provided the applicant meets all other qualifications and
11 requirementsofthischapter,theapplicantshallberegisteredandgrantedalicensebytheofficeof
12 professional licensure and certification upon payment of the required fee and presentation of
13 satisfactory proof that the applicant has passed the parts of such [national examination]
14 examinations.
15 2 EffectiveDate. Thisactshalltakeeffect60daysafteritspassage.
