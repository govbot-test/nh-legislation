METHODOLOGY:
This bill requires the Department of Health and Human Services to seek approval from the
federal Centers for Medicare and Medicaid Services (CMS) to include family caregiver support
services under the Acquired Brain Disorder (ABD) the Choices for Independence (CFI) waiver
programs. In addition, the bill eliminates the child care scholarship cost share for children in
kinship care. As amended, the bill requires the Department to seek Fiscal Committee approval
for funding relative to the components of the bill that are expected to have a fiscal impact. Since
anyexpenditures will bedetermined by this subsequent action of theFiscal Committee, they are
identified as indeterminablein the tableabove. The Department has provided the following cost
estimates.
· The addition of family caregiver support services is anticipated to cost $226,000 in the
first year, increasing to $232,000 in the second. The Department assumes that coverage
of these services will go into effect in FY28, as time will be needed for implementation.
These services will be covered with 50 percent general funds and 50 percent federal
funds.
· Systems changes to the New Heights eligibility system will cost $310,000 in FY27, and
befullygenerallyfunded.
· The requirement that cost share be eliminated for children in kinship care is expected to
decreaserevenue(indirectlyincreasinggeneralfundcosts)by$10,000peryear.
In addition to the changes above, the bill adds retirement as an activity that counts toward the
child care scholarship work requirement. In response to the bill as introduced, the Department
noted that these provisions may run afoul of federal regulations, potentially jeopardizing federal
funds. Asamended,however,thebillmakesenactmentoftheprovisionscontingentuponfederal
approval. Forthisreason,thesecomponentsofthebillareexpectedtohavenofiscalimpact.
