ANACT enabling the use of solid waste management funds by solid waste management
districts.
BeitEnactedbytheSenateandHouseofRepresentativesinGeneralCourtconvened:
1 1 Purpose and Use of the Solid Waste Management Fund. Amend RSA 149-R:4, II to read as
2 follows:
3 II. The fund shall be used to provide quarterly payments to New Hampshire municipalities
4 or New Hampshire solid waste management districts established under RSA 53-B for source
5 reduction and recycling efforts to offset payments made by the municipality or applicable solid
6 waste management district associated with the solid waste disposal surcharge established under
7 RSA 149-R:5, based upon the tonnage of solid waste for which the municipality or solid waste
8 management district was financially responsible for disposal at a New Hampshire landfill,
9 incinerator, or waste-to-energy facility. Payments to solid waste districts shall reflect only the
10 amount of the surcharge paid for waste generated by New Hampshire municipalities
11 within the applicable solid waste district. Administration of the payment program shall be in
12 accordance with procedures established by rulemaking under the authority of RSA 149-R:6, IV and
13 V. Such rulemaking shall specifically address the unique circumstances for solid waste
14 managementdistrictsor municipalitiesthatownandoperateafacilitythatissubjecttoRSA149-
15 R:5, or municipalities that are part of a solid waste district that owns and operates such a facility
16 toensurethatthecostsincurredbythosemunicipalitiesorsolidwastemanagementdistrictsare
17 offsetconsistentwiththischapter.
18 2 SolidWasteManagementFund;Rulemaking. AmendRSA149-R:6,IV-Vtoreadasfollows:
19 IV. The time, amount, and manner of payments to New Hampshire municipalities and
20 solidwastemanagementdistrictspursuanttoRSA149-R:4,II.
21 V. Certified reports required to be submitted by municipalities and solid waste
22 managementdistrictsrequestingpaymentspursuanttoRSA149-R:4,II.
23 3 EffectiveDate. Thisactshalltakeeffectuponitspassage.

METHODOLOGY:
This bill allows solid waste management districts, in addition to municipalities, to pay the solid
waste disposal surcharge and to receive reimbursement from the Solid Waste Management
Fund. TheDepartmentofEnvironmentalServicesstatesanysurchargepaymentsmadebysolid
waste management districts would be deposited into the Solid Waste Management Fund and
that such districts would be eligible for reimbursement of 100% of the surcharge paid from the
same fund. As a result, the Department states the fiscal impact on state revenues and
expenditures is expected to be neutral, as any increase in revenue would be offset by a
correspondingincreaseinexpendituresfromthesamefund.
The fiscal impact on municipalities is expected to be neutral. Solid waste management districts
and municipalities would be required to pay the solid waste disposal surcharge; however, they
would be eligible for reimbursement from the Solid Waste Management Fund for amounts paid,
resultinginnonetfiscalimpact.
