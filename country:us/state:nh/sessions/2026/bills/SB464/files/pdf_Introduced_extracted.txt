STATEOFNEWHAMPSHIRE
IntheYearofOurLordTwoThousandTwenty-Six
ANACT relativetocivilrightsenforcement.
BeitEnactedbytheSenateandHouseofRepresentativesinGeneralCourtconvened:
1 1 CivilRightsAct;CivilRightsEnforcement. AmendRSA354-B:1,Itoreadasfollows:
2 I. All persons have the right to engage in lawful activities and to exercise and enjoy the
3 rights secured by the United States and New Hampshire Constitutions and the laws of the United
4 States and New Hampshire without being subject to actual or threatened physical force or violence
5 againstthemoranyotherpersonorbyactualorthreateneddamagetoortrespassonpropertywhen
6 such actual or threatened conduct is substantially motivated by hostility towards the victim's
7 race, color, religion, national origin, ancestry, sexual orientation, sex, gender identity, or disability.
8 "Threatened physical force" and "threatened damage to or trespass on property" is a communication,
9 by physical conduct or by declaration, of an intent to inflict harm on a person or a person's property
10 bysomeunlawfulactwithapurposetoterrorizeorcoerce.
11 2 EffectiveDate. ThisactshalltakeeffectJanuary1,2027.

METHODOLOGY:
This bill adds, deletes, or modifies a criminal penalty, or changes statute to which there is a
penalty for violation. Therefore, this bill may have an impact on the judicial and correctional
systems, which could affect prosecution, incarceration, probation, and parole costs, for the state,
as well as county and local governments. A summary of such costs can be found at:
https://gencourt.state.nh.us/lba/Budget/Fiscal_Notes/JudicialCorrectionalCosts.pdf
