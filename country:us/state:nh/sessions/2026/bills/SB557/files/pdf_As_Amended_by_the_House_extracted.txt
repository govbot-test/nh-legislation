557-FN
ANACT making it illegal for liquor commission licensees to sell, distribute, furnish, offer
for sale, advertise, manufacture, possess, or allow the ingestion, inhalation,
smoking,orvapingofcertainkratomproducts.
SPONSORS: Sen. Altschiller, Dist 24; Sen. Watters, Dist 4; Sen. Prentiss, Dist 5; Rep. Manos,
Rock.12;Rep.N.Murphy,Hills.12
COMMITTEE: Judiciary
─────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
AMENDEDANALYSIS
This bill makes it illegal for liquor commission licensees to sell, distribute, furnish, offer for sale,
advertise, manufacture, possess, or allow the ingestion, inhalation, smoking, or vaping of certain
kratomproducts.

STATEOFNEWHAMPSHIRE
IntheYearofOurLordTwoThousandTwenty-Six
ANACT making it illegal for liquor commission licensees to sell, distribute, furnish, offer
for sale, advertise, manufacture, possess, or allow the ingestion, inhalation,
smoking,orvapingofcertainkratomproducts.
BeitEnactedbytheSenateandHouseofRepresentativesinGeneralCourtconvened:
1 1 NewParagraph;AlcoholicBeverages;DefinitionsandGeneralProvisions;Definitions. Amend
2 RSA175:1byinsertingafterparagraphLXIV-gthefollowingnewparagraph:
3 LXIV-h. "Unauthorized Kratom Products" means any consumable kratom product,
4 including,butnotlimitedto,edibles,liquids,powders,capsules,tablets,extracts,inhalants,orvapor
5 productsthatfallintooneormoreofthefollowingcategories:
6 (a) An alkaloid or alkaloid derivative contained in a kratom leaf extract that has been
7 exposed to chemicals or processes that confer a structural change in the alkaloids, including, but not
8 limited to, oxidation, reduction, and ring opening or closing, resulting in material that has been
9 chemicallyaltered.
10 (b) An alkaloid or alkaloid derivative of the kratom leaf that has been created by
11 chemical synthesis or biosynthetic means, including fermentation, recombinant techniques, yeast-
12 driven techniques, and enzymatic techniques, rather than by traditional food preparation
13 techniques,suchasheatingorextracting.
14 (c) Purified mitragynine with enhanced levels of 7-hydroxymitragynine that are not
15 foundinthenaturalkratomleaf.
16 2 Enforcement, Requirements and Penalties; Premises Restrictions; Unlawful Purpose. RSA
17 179:50isrepealedandreenactedtoreadasfollows:
18 179:50 UnlawfulPurpose.
19 I. Nolicenseeshalluse,orallowtobeused,theirpremisesforanypurposecontrarytolaw.
20 II. No licensee shall permit or allow the on-premises ingestion, inhalation, smoking, or
21 vaping of any unauthorized kratom products, as defined by RSA 175:1, LXIV -h, on any licensed
22 premises.
23 III. No licensee shall sell, distribute, furnish, offer for sale, advertise, manufacture, or
24 possessanyunauthorizedkratomproductsasdefinedbyRSA175:1,LXIV-h.
25 IV. The penalty for violations of paragraph II or paragraph III shall be determined by the
26 state liquor commission in accordance with rules adopted under RSA 541-A. However, a second
27 offenseunderparagraphIIorparagraphIIIshallresultinrevocationofthelicense.
28 3 EffectiveDate. ThisactshalltakeeffectJanuary1,2027.

SB557-FN-FISCALNOTE
ASAMENDEDBYTHEHOUSE(AMENDMENT#2026-1473h)
ANACT making it illegal for liquor commission licensees to sell, distribute, furnish, offer
for sale, advertise, manufacture, possess, or allow the ingestion, inhalation,
smoking,orvapingofcertainkratomproducts.
FISCALIMPACT:
This bill makes it illegal for Liquor Commission licensees to sell, distribute, furnish, offer for
sale, advertise, manufacture, possess, or allow the use of certain unauthorized kratom products.
The Liquor Commission states the requirements of this bill could be absorbed into their current
budget. To the extent penalties are imposed for violations, there may be an indeterminable
increase in state revenue; however, any such impact is expected to be minimal and would be
attributable to rules and not this bill. Therefore, the Office of Legislative Budget Assistant
statesthisbillhasnofiscalimpactonstate,countyandlocalexpendituresorrevenue.
