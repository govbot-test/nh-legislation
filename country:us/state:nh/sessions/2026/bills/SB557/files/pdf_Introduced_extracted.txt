METHODOLOGY:
This bill creates a regulatory framework for the sale of kratom products in the state. It prohibits
sales to individuals under 21, requires licensure for all manufacturers, importers, distributors,
and retailers operating in or into the state, and establishes product safety, labeling, and
packagingstandards. Thebillauthorizesenforcementactionsincludingfines,licenserevocation,
productseizure,anddestructionofnoncompliantgoods. ItdirectstheDepartmentofHealthand
Human Services to administer the program, including inspections, hearings, and rulemaking,
and establishes a dedicated Kratom Administration Account Fund to receive licensing fees and
penalties. The bill takes effect January 1, 2027, with licensing and enforcement beginning April
1,2027.
The bill establishes licensing fees of $2,500 for manufacturers, importers, and distributors and
$1,500 for retailers from April 1, 2027 through December 31, 2029. The Department of Health
and Human Services estimates annual revenue between $275,000 to $612,500, based on
projections of 150 to 350 retailers, 10 to 15 manufacturers or distributors, and minimal fine
revenue. (Given the bill's effective date, the it is assumed that 25 percent of this amount will be
collected in FY27, with the full amount collected in later years.) The Department expects,
however, that costs will exceed revenues, particularly in the first year of implementation.
Estimated costs range from $1.75 million to $2 million in FY27, largely due to one-time IT
system development costs of $1to$1.25 million, plus ongoingstaffing, indirect costs, and system
maintenance. The Department expects that staffing needs will necessitate hiring one
supervisor, three or more compliance managers, and an IT developer/programmer, at a cost of
$580,000+ per year. The Department also anticipates that, due to its unfamiliarity with the law
enforcement activities required by the bill, it will need to expand upon its current contract with
the NH Liquor Commission, which is currently limited to tobacco enforcement. In total, the
Department expects that ongoing operational costs will be $855,000 in FY28 and $891,000 in
FY29. For the purposes of this fiscal note, it is assumed that any costs not covered by the
dedicatedKratomfundwillbepaidforwithstategeneralfunds.

METHODOLOGY:
This bill creates a regulatory framework for the sale of kratom products in the state. It prohibits
sales to individuals under 21, requires licensure for all manufacturers, importers, distributors,
and retailers operating in or into the state, and establishes product safety, labeling, and
packagingstandards. Thebillauthorizesenforcementactionsincludingfines,licenserevocation,
productseizure,anddestructionofnoncompliantgoods. ItdirectstheDepartmentofHealthand
Human Services to administer the program, including inspections, hearings, and rulemaking,
and establishes a dedicated Kratom Administration Account Fund to receive licensing fees and
penalties. The bill takes effect January 1, 2027, with licensing and enforcement beginning April
1,2027.
The bill establishes licensing fees of $2,500 for manufacturers, importers, and distributors and
$1,500 for retailers from April 1, 2027 through December 31, 2029. The Department of Health
and Human Services estimates annual revenue between $275,000 to $612,500, based on
projections of 150 to 350 retailers, 10 to 15 manufacturers or distributors, and minimal fine
revenue. (Given the bill's effective date, the it is assumed that 25 percent of this amount will be
collected in FY27, with the full amount collected in later years.) The Department expects,
however, that costs will exceed revenues, particularly in the first year of implementation.
Estimated costs range from $1.75 million to $2 million in FY27, largely due to one-time IT

system development costs of $1to$1.25 million, plus ongoingstaffing, indirect costs, and system
maintenance. The Department expects that staffing needs will necessitate hiring one
supervisor, three or more compliance managers, and an IT developer/programmer, at a cost of
$580,000+ per year. The Department also anticipates that, due to its unfamiliarity with the law
enforcement activities required by the bill, it will need to expand upon its current contract with
the NH Liquor Commission, which is currently limited to tobacco enforcement. In total, the
Department expects that ongoing operational costs will be $855,000 in FY28 and $891,000 in
FY29. For the purposes of this fiscal note, it is assumed that any costs not covered by the
dedicatedKratomfundwillbepaidforwithstategeneralfunds.
