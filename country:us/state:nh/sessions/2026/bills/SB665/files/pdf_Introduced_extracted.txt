26-3266

STATEOFNEWHAMPSHIRE
IntheYearofOurLordTwoThousandTwenty-Six
ANACT requiring pharmacies to charge consumers the lowest available price for
prescriptiondrugs.
BeitEnactedbytheSenateandHouseofRepresentativesinGeneralCourtconvened:
1 1 New Paragraph; Pharmacists and Pharmacies; Pharmacy Board; Rulemaking Authority.
2 AmendRSA318:5-abyinsertingafterparagraphXXthefollowingnewparagraph:
3 XXI. Procedures for verifying compliance, consumer complaints, and enforcement actions
4 broughtpursuanttoRSA318:47-nthroughRSA318:47-q.
5 2 New Subdivision; Prescription Drug Prices. Amend RSA 318 by inserting after section 47-m
6 thefollowingnewsubdivision:
7 PrescriptionDrugPrices
8 318:47-n Definitions. Inthissection:
9 I. "Pharmacy retail price" means the cash price a consumer without prescription drug
10 coverageorotherdiscountswouldpayatapharmacy,excludinganypharmacistdispensingfee.
11 II. "Cost-sharing amount" means the amount owed by a covered individual under the terms
12 ofahealthinsurancepolicyorasrequiredbyapharmacybenefitmanager.
13 III. "Discountedprice"meansanypubliclyavailableorpharmacy-affiliateddiscount,coupon,
14 orsavingsprogrampriceavailabletoaconsumeratthetimeofsale.
15 IV. "Lowest available price" means the lowest of the following prices for a prescription
16 medicationasdeterminedatthetimeofsale:
17 (a) Thepharmacyretailprice;
18 (b) The consumer's cost-sharing amount under insurance or a pharmacy benefit
19 managerarrangement;or
20 (c) Any discounted price available through the pharmacy or a recognized discount
21 program.
22 318:47-o DutytoChargeLowestAvailablePrice.
23 I. A pharmacist or pharmacy employee shall, at the point of sale, determine the lowest
24 available price for the prescription medication being dispensed and shall charge the consumer that
25 lowestavailableprice.
26 II. The pharmacist or pharmacy employee shall inform the consumer of the lowest available
27 pricebeforecompletingthetransaction.
28 III. No insurer, pharmacy benefit manager, or third-party payer shall restrict, prohibit, or
29 penalizeapharmacistfordisclosingorchargingthelowestavailablepricetoaconsumer.
SB665-FN-ASINTRODUCED
-Page2-
1 IV. If a consumer elects to use insurance, despite a lower cash or discount price, the
2 pharmacyshalldocumenttheconsumer'selectionatthepointofsale.
3 318:47-p EnforcementandPenalties.
4 I. A violation of this subdivision shall constitute unprofessional conduct under RSA 318 and
5 shallsubjectthelicenseetodisciplinaryactionbytheboardofpharmacy.
6 II. The board may impose a civil administrative penalty not to exceed $1,000 for each
7 violation.
8 III. Consumersshallnotbeliableforanyamountexceedingthelowestavailablepriceatthe
9 timeofsale.
10 318:47-q Rulemaking. The board of pharmacy shall adopt rules under RSA 541-A as necessary
11 to implement the provisions of this subdivision, including procedures for verifying compliance,
12 consumercomplaints,andenforcementactions.
13 3 EffectiveDate. ThisactshalltakeeffectJanuary1,2027.
