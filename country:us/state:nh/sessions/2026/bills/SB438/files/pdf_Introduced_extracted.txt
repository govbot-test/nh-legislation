SB438-ASINTRODUCED
26-2240
08/09
STATEOFNEWHAMPSHIRE
IntheYearofOurLordTwoThousandTwenty-Six
ANACT enabling the department of safety and the secretary of state to develop a process
tosharecertaininformationregardingREALIDapplicants.
BeitEnactedbytheSenateandHouseofRepresentativesinGeneralCourtconvened:
1 1 New Section; Identification. Amend RSA659byinsertingafter section13-bthefollowingnew
2 section:
3 659:13-c CitizenshipInformation;SecretaryofState;DepartmentofSafety.
4 I. Notwithstanding any other provision of law, any United States citizen applying for an
5 enhanced identification card pursuant to RSA 260:21-b shall be given the opportunity to have their
6 photo image and information regarding their United States citizenship shared with the secretary of
7 state for voting identification purposes. The commissioner of the department of safety and the
8 secretary of state shall develop an agreement to electronically transmit photo images and
9 informationrelatedtoUnitedStatescitizenshipsecurelyandconfidentiallybyJanuary1,2027.
10 II. The secretary of state shall transmit photo images and information related to United
11 States citizenship to the appropriate clerk to be used by the clerk, moderator, or supervisor of the
12 checklist for purposes of identification, if applicable. The secretary of state shall transmit photo
13 images and other United States citizenship information to the appropriate clerk within 3 days of
14 receiptofsuchphotosandinformation. Notwithstandinganyotherprovisionoflaw,thephotoimage
15 and United States citizenship information provided shall satisfy proof of citizenship documentation
16 requirements pursuant to RSA 654:7-a and RSA 654:12. In order to be used on election day, the
17 photoimageandUnitedStatescitizenshipinformationshallbecapturedbythedepartmentofsafety
18 nolaterthan14daysbeforetheelection.
19 III. In cities and towns that use electronic poll books under RSA 652:27, the clerk shall
20 download the photo image and United States citizenship information to the checklist no later than
21 the Friday before the election. In cities and towns that do not use electronic poll books, the clerk
22 shall confirm receipt of the photo image and United States citizenship information by marking the
23 column on the checklist for citizenship information under RSA 654:25. Nothing in this section shall
24 be construed as permitting the electronic poll book device to be connected to the Internet on election
25 day.
26 IV. The applicant may decline or withdraw consent to have his or her photo and United
27 States citizenship information shared with the secretary of state at any time by contacting the
28 department of safety. In the event the applicant declines or withdraws consent, the department of
29 safety and the secretary of state shall remove the photo image captured for the enhanced
SB438 -ASINTRODUCED
-Page2-
1 identification card promptly and the secretary of state shall remove information regarding the
2 applicant'sUnitedStatescitizenshippromptly.
3 V. Nothing in this section shall be construed as enabling the registering of an applicant at
4 anofficeofthedepartmentofsafetyorimpactinganapplicant'svoterregistrationstatus.
5 2 PreparingChecklist. AmendRSA654:25toreadasfollows:
6 654:25 Preparing Checklist. The secretary of state shall issue and distribute guidelines for the
7 content, composition, and style of checklists and for the maintenance of data related to checklists by
8 which the supervisors of the checklist shall compile and correct the checklist. Such guidelines shall
9 specify the information which will be maintained and updated by the supervisors. The secretary
10 shall establish standard forms and procedures for the use of the supervisors for the maintenance of
11 such information. The information to be maintained and updated shall include the full name,
12 domicile address, mailing address, citizenship status, if the clerk has received such
13 information from the secretary of state under RSA 659:13-c, and party affiliation, if any, of
14 each voter on the checklist and such other information as the secretary requires. Every checklist
15 used at any election shall at a minimum contain the full name, domicile address, mailing address,
16 andpartyaffiliation,ifany,ofeachvoteronthechecklist. Thepaperchecklistsusedbyballotclerks
17 on election day need not include mailing addresses. The name and address of a voter shall not
18 appear on the checklist at the request of the voter if the voter presents to the supervisors of the
19 checklist a valid protective order pursuant to RSA 173-B. The name, domicile address, and mailing
20 address, if different, of such a voter shall be maintained on a separate list of voters, which shall be
21 nonpublic and not subject to RSA 91-A. If it is necessary to establish such a nonpublic list, the
22 publicchecklist shall bemarked at the end withanotationof thenumber of voters whose names are
23 maintainedonthenonpubliclist.
24 3 EffectiveDate. ThisactshalltakeeffectJanuary1,2027.
