SB669-FN-ASAMENDEDBYTHESENATE
03/12/2026 1021s
2026SESSION
26-3269

669-FN
ANACT relativetoon-premiseslicensesforlicensedbarbershopsandsalons.
SPONSORS: Sen. Perkins Kwoka, Dist 21; Sen. Prentiss, Dist 5; Sen. Murphy, Dist 16; Sen.
Pearl, Dist 17; Sen. Ward, Dist 8; Sen. Innis, Dist 7; Sen. Watters, Dist 4; Sen.
Rochefort,Dist1;Sen.Avard,Dist12
COMMITTEE: Commerce
─────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
AMENDEDANALYSIS
This increases separates the on-premises license for barbershops and salons into two tiers, one
which allows for a single sale and one which allows for service without charge, and requires the
liquorcommissiontomakeanannualreporttothelegislatureregardingtheissuance oflicenses and
thenumberofviolationswhichoccurred.

03/12/2026 1021s 26-3269
08/09
STATEOFNEWHAMPSHIRE
IntheYearofOurLordTwoThousandTwenty-Six
ANACT relativetoon-premiseslicensesforlicensedbarbershopsandsalons.
BeitEnactedbytheSenateandHouseofRepresentativesinGeneralCourtconvened:
1 1 On-PremisesLicense;SalonorBarbershop. AmendRSA178:21,IIItoreadasfollows:
2 III. Thecommissionmayissuealicensetoabusinesscurrentlylicensedthroughtheofficeof
3 professional licensure and certification, pursuant to RSA 313-A:19, as a salon or barbershop, as
4 defined under RSA 313-A:1, to serve one alcoholic beverage to customers who are receiving services
5 from said establishment. Such licenses shall be designated either as a tier 1 license or a tier
6 2license.
7 (a)(1) Notwithstanding the provisions of RSA 179:44, a tier 1 licensee under this
8 paragraph may serve alcoholic beverages and liquor [for free] without charge for on-premises
9 consumption, which shall be limited to one drink per customer in the amount not to exceed one 16-
10 ounce glass of beverage, 6-ounce glass of wine, or 1 1/2 ounces of liquor per person per
11 appointment.
12 (2) Thefeeforatier1licenseunderthisparagraphshallbe$100.
13 (3) The licensee shall keep records of the patron served. Such records shall be
14 retainedbythelicensedfacilityandshallbemadeavailabletothecommissionuponrequest.
15 (b)(1) [ The fee for the license under this paragraph shall be $100.] A tier 2 licensee
16 may sell alcoholic beverages and liquor for on-premises consumption only, which shall be
17 limited to one drink per customer in the amount not to exceed one 16-ounce glass of
18 beverage,6-ounceglassofwine,or11/2ouncesofliquorperpersonperappointment.
19 (2) Thefeeforatier2licenseshallbe$480.
20 (3) Sales authorized under this paragraph shall be incidental to the
21 provision of barbering or cosmetology services and shall not constitute a standalone bar or
22 restaurantoperation.
23 (4) The licensee shall keep records of alcoholic beverages sold under this
24 paragraph, which shall be retained by the licensed facility and shall be made available to
25 thecommissionuponrequest.
26 (c) Nothing in this section shall allow the commission to issue a license to a salon or
27 barbershoplocatedwithinaprivateresidence.
28 (d) For the purposes of this section, persons under the age of 21 years shall be
29 allowedinthesalonbutshallnotbeofferednorconsumeanyalcoholicbeverageorliquor.
30 (e) The licensee shall complete the management training seminar offered by the
31 commission.
SB669-FN-ASAMENDEDBYTHESENATE
-Page2-
1 (f) The liquor commission shall submit an annual report to the speaker of the
2 house of representatives and the president of the senate by November 1 of each year listing
3 the number of licenses applied for, granted, and denied, the number of inspections
4 performed,andthetotalnumberofviolationswhichoccurredwithinthereportingyear.
5 2 EffectiveDate. Thisactshalltakeeffect60daysafteritspassage.
