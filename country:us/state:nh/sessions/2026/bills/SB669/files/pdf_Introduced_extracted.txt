SB669-FN-ASINTRODUCED
2026SESSION
26-3269
08/09
669-FN
ANACT relativetoon-premiseslicensesforlicensedbarbershopsandsalons.
SPONSORS: Sen. Perkins Kwoka, Dist 21; Sen. Prentiss, Dist 5; Sen. Murphy, Dist 16; Sen.
Pearl, Dist 17; Sen. Ward, Dist 8; Sen. Innis, Dist 7; Sen. Watters, Dist 4; Sen.
Rochefort,Dist1;Sen.Avard,Dist12
COMMITTEE: Commerce
─────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
ANALYSIS
This increases the fee for an on-premises license for barbershops and salons, requires the liquor
commission to inspect such licensees, and requires the liquor commission to make an annual report
to the legislature regarding the issuance of licenses and the number and type of violations which
occurred.

3 professional licensure and certification, pursuant to RSA 313-A:19, as a salon or barbershop, as
4 defined under RSA 313-A:1, to serve one alcoholic beverage to customers who are receiving services
5 fromsaidestablishment.
6 (a) Notwithstanding the provisions of RSA 179:44, a licensee under this paragraph may
7 serve alcoholic beverages and liquor for free, which shall be limited to one drink per customer in the
8 amount not to exceed one 16-ounce glass of beverage, 6-ounce glass of wine, or 1 1/2 ounces of liquor
9 per person per appointment. The licensee shall keep records of the patron served. Such records
10 shall be retained by the licensed facility and shall be made available to the commission upon
11 request.
12 (b) Thefeeforthelicenseunderthisparagraphshallbe[$100]$480.
13 (c) Nothing in this section shall allow the commission to issue a license to a salon or
14 barbershoplocatedwithinaprivateresidence.
15 (d) For the purposes of this section, persons under the age of 21 years shall be
16 allowedinthesalonbutshallnotbeofferednorconsumeanyalcoholicbeverageorliquor.
17 (e) The licensee and all of his or her employees shall complete responsible
18 beverageservicetrainingofferedbythecommissionandshallkeeprecordsofcompletionof
19 such training which shall be shown to the liquor commission upon request. The liquor
20 commissionshallperformanannualinspectionofthelicensee'sfacility.
21 (f) The liquor commission shall submit an annual report to the speaker of the
22 house of representatives and the president of the senate by November 1 of each year listing
23 the number of licenses applied for, granted, and denied, the number of inspections
24 performed,andthetotalnumberofviolationswhichoccurredwithinthereportingyear.
25 2 EffectiveDate. Thisactshalltakeeffect60daysafteritspassage.
