sideofInterstate93,betweenPillsburyRoadandtheExit4Southboundoff-ramp.

SB631-FN-ASINTRODUCED
26-2221
06/05
STATEOFNEWHAMPSHIRE
IntheYearofOurLordTwoThousandTwenty-Six
ANACT installingasoundbarrieronInterstate93southbound.
BeitEnactedbytheSenateandHouseofRepresentativesinGeneralCourtconvened:
1 1 Department of Transportation; I-93 Southbound Installation of Sound Barriers. The
2 department of transportation shall install sound barriers beginning at Pillsbury Road in
3 Londonderry, New Hampshire and running south along the west side of Interstate 93 bordering the
4 Woodmont Development property line for a distance of approximately 1,845 feet to the Route 102
5 exitramp(Exit4)uptothestonecutcliffalongtheexitramp.
6 2 EffectiveDate. Thisactshalltakeeffectuponitspassage.

METHODOLOGY:
This bill would require the Department of Transportation to construct sound barriers along the
I-93 southbound side between the Pillsbury Road and the I-93 Exit 4 southbound off-ramp. The
Departmentstatesthebarriersareexpectedtobeabout1,845feetlongand25feethigh,totaling
roughly 46,125 square feet, at an estimated cost of $8,071,875, based on an estimated cost of
$175 per square foot. The Department states federal funding could only be used if the project
meets state noise policy requirements, otherwise it would need full state or alternative funding.
If federal eligibility is confirmed and the bill is enacted, the project would displace another
project,orprojects,currentlyintheTen-YearTransportationImprovementPlan.
