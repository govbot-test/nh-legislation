STATEOFNEWHAMPSHIRE
IntheYearofOurLordTwoThousandTwenty-Six
ANACT establishing a commission to study the implementation of a residential builder
registrationsystem.
BeitEnactedbytheSenateandHouseofRepresentativesinGeneralCourtconvened:
1 1 Findings and Statement of Purpose. The general court finds that the New Hampshire
2 residential home building industry is essential to New Hampshire’s economic vitality, workforce
3 development, and housing availability. The general court also finds that while the overwhelming
4 majority of New Hampshire builders operate with integrity and professionalism, isolated instances
5 of fraud and abuse, often from out of state, have caused harm to consumers and undermined
6 confidence in the industry. The general court recognizes that leaders within the New Hampshire
7 residential building community have expressed a commitment to strengthening accountability
8 measures and supporting reasonable mechanisms to promote transparency and consumer protection
9 withoutcreatingunnecessaryfinancialorregulatoryburdens.
10 2 New Subdivision; Commission to Study the Implementation of a Residential Builder
11 Registration System. Amend RSA 310-A by inserting after section 222 the following new
12 subdivision:
13 CommissiontoStudytheImplementationofaResidentialBuilderRegistrationSystem
14 310-A:223 CommissionEstablished;Duties;Report.
15 I. Thereis established acommissiontostudytheimplementationofastatewideregistration
16 systemforresidentialbuildersandcontractors.
17 II. For the purposes of this section, “residential builder” shall mean a person or business
18 entity whose primary source of income is derived from the construction, reconstruction, or
19 substantial renovation of residential properties consisting of 4 or fewer dwelling units in a single
20 building.
21 III. NotwithstandingRSA14:49,themembersofthecommissionshallbeasfollows:
22 (a) Onememberofthesenate,appointedbythepresidentofthesenate.
23 (b) Two members of the house of representatives, appointed by the speaker of the house
24 ofrepresentatives,atleastoneofwhomshallhaveexperienceinmunicipaladministration.
25 (c) Onememberfromthedepartmentofjustice,appointedbytheattorneygeneral.
26 (d) Onememberfromthedepartmentoflabor,appointedbythecommissioneroflabor.
27 (e) One member from the office of professional licensure and certification, appointed by
28 thedirector.
29 (f) Two members with experience in residential home building, appointed by the New
30 HampshireHomeBuildersAssociation.
SB523-FN-ASAMENDEDBYTHESENATE
-Page2-
1 (g) The chief executive officer of the New Hampshire Home Builders Association, or the
2 chiefexecutiveofficer’sdesignee.
3 IV. Legislativemembersofthecommissionshallreceivemileageatthelegislativeratewhen
4 attendingtothedutiesofthecommission.
5 V. Thecommissionshall:
6 (a) Examine the viability, process, and necessity of implementing a statewide
7 registrationforresidentialbuilders.
8 (b) Assess whether a registration system would enhance consumer protection and
9 addressinstancesoffraud,misrepresentation,exploitation,andmiscommunication.
10 (c) Evaluate the scope and structure of a registration system, including eligibility
11 requirements, renewal standards, complaint procedures, fee structures, oversight, and disciplinary
12 mechanisms.
13 (d) Identify which, if any, existing state agency or department would be best suited to
14 oversee and administer a registration system, including but not limited to the department of labor,
15 theofficeofprofessionallicensureandcertification,orthedepartmentofjustice.
16 (e) Solicit testimony from members of the public, representatives of the residential
17 building industry, municipal officials, consumer protection advocates, and other interested
18 stakeholders.
19 (f) Recommendafeestructuresufficienttofullyfundadministrationwithoutrelianceon
20 generalfunds.
21 VI. The first-named senate member shall call the first meeting of the commission within 45
22 days of the effective date of this section. The commission shall elect a chairperson from among its
23 membersatitsfirstmeeting. Fivemembersofthecommissionshallconstituteaquorum.
24 VII. The commission shall submit a report of its findings and any recommendations for
25 proposed legislation to the speaker of the house of representatives, the president of the senate, the
26 chairs of the house and senate commerce committees, and the governor on or before November 30,
27 2026.
28 3 Repeal. RSA 310-A:223, and the subdivision heading preceding it, relative to commission to
29 studytheimplementationofaresidentialbuilderregistrationsystem,isrepealed.
30 4 EffectiveDate.
31 I. Section3ofthisactshalltakeeffectNovember30,2026.
32 II. Theremainderofthisactshalltakeeffectuponitspassage.
