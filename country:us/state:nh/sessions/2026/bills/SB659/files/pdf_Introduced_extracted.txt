26-3264
12/08
STATEOFNEWHAMPSHIRE
IntheYearofOurLordTwoThousandTwenty-Six
ANACT relativetoeducationfinancing.
BeitEnactedbytheSenateandHouseofRepresentativesinGeneralCourtconvened:
1 1 Education; Adequate Public Education; Policy and Purpose. RSA 193-E:1, I-II is repealed and
2 reenactedtoreadasfollows:
3 I. It is the policy of the state of New Hampshire that public elementary and secondary
4 education shall provide all students with the opportunity to acquire the knowledge and skills
5 necessary to prepare them for successful participation in the social, economic, scientific,
6 technological,andpoliticalsystemsofafreegovernment,nowandintheyearstocome;aneducation
7 that is consistent with the state-established academic standards, and school district or school
8 curriculum.
9 II. Respecting New Hampshire's long tradition of community involvement, it is the purpose
10 of this chapter to ensure that appropriate means are established to provide an adequate education
11 through an integrated public education system of shared responsibility between state and local
12 government,inrecognitionofthefact thatlocal governmental entities,includingschool districts, are
13 created by the state, receive their authority to act and operate from the state, and are therefore
14 indivisible from the state for the purposes of providing an adequate education. In this system, the
15 state and local governmental entities function collectively to ensure that an adequate version of the
16 academicprogramdefinedinRSA193-E:2-aisprovidedtoNewHampshirepupils. Suchasystemof
17 shared responsibility ensures flexibility in implementing diverse educational approaches to
18 instruction and curriculum tailored to meet student needs, allows local governments to make the
19 local infrastructure and administrative choices that are best for their particular communities, and
20 helps ensure that local school districts remain accountable and responsive to community and family
21 needs. How the state and its local governmental entities choose to raise, allocate, and spend
22 financial resources to implement this integrated public education system is a political policy matter
23 reservedtolegislativeandexecutivejudgmentandcontrol.
24 2 Education; Adequate Public Education; Substantive Educational Content of an Adequate
25 Education. AmendRSA193-E:2-a,I(a)toreadasfollows:
26 I.(a) [Beginning in the school year 2008-2009, and for each year thereafter, the specific
27 criteria and substantive educational program that deliver the opportunity for an] An adequate
28 education shall be defined and identified as the school approval standards in the following learning
29 areas:
30 (1) English/languageartsandreading.
31 (2) Mathematics.

2 (4) Social studies, including civics, government, economics, geography, history, and

6 (7) Health and wellness education, including a policy for violations of RSA 126-K:8,

12 3 Education; School Money; Cost of an Opportunity for an Adequate Education. RSA 198:40-a,
