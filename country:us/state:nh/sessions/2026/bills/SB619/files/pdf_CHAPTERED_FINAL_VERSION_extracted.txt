2 toreadasfollows:
3 (3) Courts shall give cases in which animals have been confiscated by an arresting
4 officer priority on the court calendar. In cases in which animals have been confiscated by an arresting
5 officerorhisorheragency,astatushearingshallbeheldbythecourtwithin14daysoftheconfiscationof
6 theanimals.
7 (A) Apurposeofthehearingshallbetodeterminewhethertheconfiscationofthe
8 animaloranimalswaslawfulinaccordancewithparagraphIV-a. Uponsuchashowing,thecourtmay
9 makeadeterminationoftemporarycustodythatisinthebestinterestoftheanimal'shealth,safety,and
10 wellbeing. Iftheconfiscatedanimalisco-ownedbythedefendant,thecourtmaytransferthecustodyof
11 theanimaltotheremainingownerorco-ownersequitablyifthecourtdeterminesthatsuchisinthebest
12 interestoftheanimal'shealth,safety,andwellbeing. Thecourtmayalsoaddressanyothermatters
13 beforeitasraisedbyeitherpartyorifitisintheinterestsofjustice.Uponrequestfromeitherpartyorby
14 orderofthecourt,thishearingmayincludethedefendant'sarraignment.Waiverofthearraignmentbythe
15 defendantshallnotwaivethe14dayhearingsetforthinthisparagraph.
16 (B) Iftheaccusedfailstoappearincourtwithoutgivingpriornoticewithin3daysof
17 thehearingdate,or,iflessthan3daysnotice,withoutevidenceofemergentcircumstances,thehearing
18 shalltakeplaceasscheduledandthecourtmayruleonanymattersbeforeit.
