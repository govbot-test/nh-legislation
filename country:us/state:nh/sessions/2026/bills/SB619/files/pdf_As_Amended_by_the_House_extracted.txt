confiscatedduetoallegationsofanimalcruelty.

STATEOFNEWHAMPSHIRE
IntheYearofOurLordTwoThousandTwenty-Six
ANACT establishingproceduresforexpeditedcourthearingsanddispositionofconfiscated
animals.
BeitEnactedbytheSenateandHouseofRepresentativesinGeneralCourtconvened:
1 1 CrueltytoAnimals;Court HearingUponConfiscationof Animals. AmendRSA644:8, IV(a)(3)
2 toreadasfollows:
3 (3) Courts shall give cases in which animals have been confiscated by an arresting
4 officer priority on the court calendar. In cases in which animals have been confiscated by an
5 arresting officer or his or her agency, a status hearing shall be held by the court within 14 days of
6 theconfiscationoftheanimals.
7 (A) A purpose of the hearing shall be to determine whether the
8 confiscation of the animal or animals was lawful in accordance with paragraph IV-a.
9 Upon such a showing, the court may make a determination of temporary custody that is in
10 the best interest of the animal's health, safety, and wellbeing. If the confiscated animal is
11 co-owned by the defendant, the court may transfer the custody of the animal to the
12 remaining owner or co-owners equitably if the court determines that such is in the best
13 interest of the animal's health, safety, and wellbeing. The court may also address any
14 other matters before it as raised by either party or if it is in the interests of justice. Upon
15 request from either party or by order of the court, this hearing may include the defendant's
16 arraignment. Waiver of the arraignment by the defendant shall not waive the 14 day
17 hearingsetforthinthisparagraph.
18 (B) If the accused fails to appear in court without giving prior notice
19 within 3 days of the hearing date, or, if less than 3 days notice, without evidence of
20 emergent circumstances, the hearing shall take place as scheduled and the court may rule
21 onanymattersbeforeit.
22 2 EffectiveDate. ThisactshalltakeeffectJanuary1,2027.
