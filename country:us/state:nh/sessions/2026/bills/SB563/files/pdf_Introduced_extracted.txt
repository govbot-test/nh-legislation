ANALYSIS
This bill requires a license for direct shippers of wine from small wineries for sale into the state
ofNewHampshire.

26-2162
08/09
STATEOFNEWHAMPSHIRE
IntheYearofOurLordTwoThousandTwenty-Six
ANACT relativetodirectshippersofwine.
BeitEnactedbytheSenateandHouseofRepresentativesinGeneralCourtconvened:
1 1 New Section; Direct Wine Shipper; License. Amend RSA 178 by inserting after section 27-b
2 thefollowingnewsection:
3 178:27-c DirectShippers;SmallWineries.
4 I. In this section, “direct wine shipper” means a winery located outside of New Hampshire
5 that produces fewer than 5,000 cases of wine in a calendar year and is licensed to ship wine directly
6 toconsumersandlicenseesinNewHampshire.
7 II. A small winery may apply to the New Hampshire liquor commission for a direct wine
8 shipperlicense. Thelicenseshallauthorizetheshipmentofwinedirectlytoconsumersandlicensees
9 within New Hampshire. The license shall be renewed annually and subject to a fee determined by
10 theliquorcommissioninrulesestablishedunderRSA541-A.
11 III. AllsalesmadebyadirectwineshipperintoNewHampshireshallincludeasurchargeof
12 25 percent of the sale price. The surcharge shall be remitted to the commission no later than the
13 15th day of the month following the month in which the sale occurred. Direct wine shippers from
14 smallwineriesshallsubmitmonthlyreportstothecommissiondetailing:
15 (a) Quantityofwinesold;
16 (b) Namesandaddressesoflicenseesandconsumerstowhomwinewasshipped;
17 (c) Priceofeachsale;
18 (d) Totalsurchargecollectedandremitted.
19 IV. All sales made under this section shall conform to RSA 179:5. The commission may
20 suspendorrevokeadirectwineshipperlicenseforviolationsofthischapterorotherapplicablelaws.
21 V. The liquor commission shall adopt rules under RSA 541-A to implement the provisions of
22 this section, including but not limited to application procedures, reporting formats, including a
23 websitetoregisterforalicense,reportsales,andremitsurcharges,andenforcementmechanisms.
24 2 EffectiveDate. ThisactshalltakeeffectJuly1,2026.

METHODOLOGY:
This bill creates a new license type for “small wineries” that produce fewer than 5,000 cases of
wine annually. The bill authorizes these wineries to ship wine directly to consumers and
licenseesinNewHampshire,subjecttoalicensefeeanda25percentsurchargeonthesaleprice
ofalldirectshipments.
TheLiquorCommissionstatesthisbillwillresultinanindeterminableincreaseinstaterevenue
from the new 25 percent surcharge on direct shipments and any associated license fees. The
Commission is unable to estimate the number of small wineries that would seek licensure or the
volumeofsalesthatwouldoccurunderthisnewcategorybutassumesitwouldbeintherangeof
$10,000 to $100,000. The Commission notes current direct shipper licensees under RSA 178:27
pay a $100 annual license fee and an 8 percent fee on the retail value of sales. The new small
winery license would replace or supplement these existing arrangements, potentially affecting
bothsurchargeandfeecollections.
