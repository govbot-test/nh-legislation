624-FN
ANACT restrictingaccesstocertainhemp-derivedproducts.
SPONSORS: Sen. Gannon, Dist 23; Rep. Litchfield, Rock. 32; Rep. M. Pearson, Rock. 34; Rep.
Bernardy,Rock.36
COMMITTEE: Judiciary
─────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
AMENDEDANALYSIS
This bill sets penalties applicable to corporations and unincorporated associations for possession
of certain hemp-derived products, directs liquor licensees to comply with the prohibition, and
amendsthedefinitionofhemp.

ANACT restrictingaccesstocertainhemp-derivedproducts.
BeitEnactedbytheSenateandHouseofRepresentativesinGeneralCourtconvened:
1 1 New Paragraph; Hemp; Definition Added. Amend RSA 439-A:2 by inserting after paragraph
2 IIthefollowingnewparagraph:
3 II-a. "Container” means the innermost wrapping, packaging, or vessel in direct contact with
4 the final hemp derived cannabinoid product when sold at retail (such as a bottle, jar, cartridge, or
5 can).
6 2 Hemp;Definition. AmendRSA439-A:2,Vtoreadasfollows:
7 V. "Hemp" means the plant Cannabis sativa L. and any part of the plant, [whether growing
8 or not, with a delta-9 tetrahydrocannabinol concentration (THC) of not more than 0.3 percent on a
9 dry weight basis] including the seeds thereof and all derivatives, extracts, cannabinoids,
10 isomers, acids, salts, and salts of isomers, whether growing or not, with a total
11 tetrahydrocannabinols concentration (including tetrahydrocannabinolic acid) of not more
12 than0.3percentonadryweightbasis.
13 3 Hemp;Prohibitions. AmendRSA439-A:4toreadasfollows:
14 439-A:4 Hemp-DerivedProductsContainingTHCProhibited.
15 I. Nothing in this chapter shall be construed to authorize the sale of products that are
16 derived from hemp which contain natural or synthetic tetrahydrocannabinol (THC) greater than 0.3
17 percentonadryweightbasis,whichappearinanyformulation,includingdelta-8THC,delta-9THC,
18 THCA,oranyotherTHCisomervariant. Suchproductsareprohibited.
19 II. Notwithstandinganyotherprovisionoflaw:
20 (a) Final hemp-derived cannabinoid products shall not include products that
21 contain more than 0.4 mg total THC per container, including all forms of
22 tetrahydrocannabinols (including delta-9 THC and THCA) and other cannabinoids with
23 similarlyintoxicatingeffects.
24 (b) Products exceeding this limit are not authorized for sale, and are excluded
25 fromthelegaldefinitionofhemp.
26 III. A corporation or unincorporated association that knowingly sells a product in
27 violation of this section shall be guilty of a violation and subject to a fine not to exceed
28 $1,000forafirst,second,orthirdoffense,andguiltyofamisdemeanorandsubjecttoafine
29 not to exceed $20,000 for any fourth or subsequent offense. A writ of execution may be
SB624-FN-ASAMENDEDBYTHEHOUSE
-Page2-
1 issuedbythecourtagainstthecorporationorunincorporatedassociationwhoviolatesthis
2 sectiontocompelpaymentofthefine,togetherwithcostsandinterest.
3 4 PremisesRestrictions;UnlawfulPurpose. AmendRSA179:50toreadasfollows:
4 179:50 UnlawfulPurpose.
5 I. No licensee shall use, or allow to be used, his or her premises for any purpose contrary to
6 law.
7 II. All licensees shall comply with the provisions of RSA 439-A:4 relative to certain
8 hemp-derivedproductscontainingTHCprohibited.
9 5 New Paragraph; Prohibited Sales. Amend RSA 179:5 by inserting after paragraph II the
10 followingnewparagraph:
11 III. No licensee, salesperson, nor any other person, shall sell or give away or cause or allow
12 or procure to be sold, delivered, or given away any product derived from hemp which contains
13 natural or synthetic tetrahydrocannabinol (THC) in any amount, which appear in any formulation,
14 including delta-8 THC, delta-9 THC, or any other THC isomer variant or tetrahydrocannabinolic
15 acid(THCA),toapersonundertheageof21.
16 6 EffectiveDate. ThisactshalltakeeffectJanuary1,2027.
